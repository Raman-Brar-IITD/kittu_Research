•ISHAN KISHAN•

"Kidnapped?" everyone gasps collectively.

The detective nods grimly.

We exchange looks of utter disbelief, numerous questions swirling in our minds: who has kidnapped him, and why would anyone target him?

The other detective speaks up. "I know you all must be shocked to hear this, but it's true."

"But why would they kidnap him? What would anyone gain by kidnapping him?" Hardik Bhai asks, slowly recovering from the initial shock.

"The thing is, they didn't intend to kidnap Mr. Gill."

Our expressions shift from shock to confusion.

"Remember that man with the wrist tattoo, the one Mr. Bumrah mistook for Mr. Gill? That person is a rival of a notorious gang. When they came to kidnap him, they ended up kidnapping Mr. Gill by mistake because of their similar appearance."

"If they kidnapped him by mistake, then why are they still holding him hostage?" Shreyas asks the detectives.

"The leader of the gang is not in India at the moment," one of them replies. "They'll only take action once he returns." She pauses and picks up her iPad. "And this is good news for us because they won't harm or do anything to him, at least not until the leader comes back."

She holds up the iPad, showing us a picture. "This is the property where they are keeping him right now," she says. The image reveals a large white bungalow, standing isolated in an otherwise deserted area.

"We have a plan, and we are going to execute it this evening," the detective declares firmly.

My heart races frantically, drowning out half of the conversation. All I hear is the blood rushing in my ears. Someone's hand lands on my shoulder, and I don’t bother to look. The squeeze is meant to be reassuring, but it only fuels my mind with the worst possible scenarios. I try to focus on the discussion happening around me.

"We also want to come," one of us says.

"No, you can't," the detective refuses.

After a heated debate, they relent slightly. "Okay, I’ll allow four or five of you, but you must stay outside. Only us and the cops will go inside for the rescue. We understand your emotions and respect them, but you all have to obey us in this situation."

________

I finally take a proper look at the bungalow and realize it has two distinct sections: a front portion that indeed looks like a 'bungalow' and a rear portion that appears crumpled and weary, desperately in need of renovation.

My phone rings, and I excuse myself from the group, starting to walk parallel to the property. Once I'm far enough from their sight, My step turn toward the back part of the bungalow- The phone ring was fake.

To reach the rear section, I have to cross a small, neglected garden, its overgrown plants and bushes obscuring the view of the back of the building.

There are some guards stationed at the front, but no one is on the other side, which is a good sign.

The dry leaves crunch under my feet as I cross the garden, the bungalow growing more eerie with each step I take. A small door waits at the top of a few worn steps, its weathered surface hinting that it hasn't been used in years.

There are also two windows situated at both ends, but they can't be seen easily as they are hidden by overgrown bushes. When I try peeking through them, I meet with disappointment.

Frustration rises within me. Maybe I should go back to where everyone is and let the detectives do their work. But then, something catches my eye. Just below the left window, there are a few dense shrubs deliberately hiding a secret passage. I can barely make out a small square cut in the wall, cleverly concealed and stuffed with a wooden block.

Trying my luck, I chop off the shrub with the help of the knife. Yes, I have a knife with me because you never know! I am grateful for my foresight in bringing it.

The hidden passage comes into view, and I gently pull at the wooden block. To my surprise, it slides out easily, revealing the entrance.

When I step through the entrance, I realize there are steps ahead. I descend carefully, discovering a basement with no light source except for the light coming from the passage behind me. Each footfall echoes in the empty space, prompting me to quickly remove my shoes. I move slowly, barely able to make out anything, but manage to situate the faint outline of steps on the far side.

Reaching those stairs, I climb up and find another door. At first, I think it is locked, but soon realize it is jammed. After a struggle that feels like fifteen minutes, I manage to force it open. I take a cautious look around. There is no one in this part, or at least it seems so. Oddly, the interior looks far more ordinary than the eerie exterior.

I carefully close the door and start taking measured steps forward, hiding behind walls and pillars. I finally reach an area where I can see two rooms: one with a digital lock and one without.

I stand near the one with the digital lock, and something within me says that they have kept him here. I press my ear against the door but hear nothing.

As I stand here, contemplating my next move, a sudden commotion from the opposite side startles me. Someone is coming. My heartbeat quickens, and I swiftly run to hide behind a nearby pillar. The footsteps grow louder, and my mouth goes dry.

Peering cautiously from behind the pillar, I see a man approach the door and start punching numbers into the digital lock. I squint my eyes to get a better look-- one-nine-one-one.

He twists the knob, and the door opens. He enters quickly. I wait patiently, my nerves taut. It doesn’t take long before he reemerges, locks the door, and leaves. I take a deep breath and start moving toward the door.

I stand there, ready to punch in the code, when a loud noise startles me. Someone has definitely seen me. I am doomed.

Slowly, I turn my head to see if anyone is behind me. To my relief, there’s no one. But then I hear more noises, this time realizing they’re coming from another part of the bungalow. The cops might have entered.

Shifting my focus back to the digital lock, I start tapping the number : 1-9-1-1.

I twist the knob, its surface cool under my palm. My insides twist with anxiety as the knob moves.

The door is open.

Slowly, I push the door. My eyes take a moment to adjust to the darkness inside. The only light filters through a small lancet window.

The room is sparse. The only furniture I can make out is a small couch.

And then I see him. He's standing beside the couch, his face turned toward the window, one hand supporting himself on the armrest. Relief surges through me, a wave of emotion so powerful it nearly knocks me off balance. I can't describe in words how much this moment means. "Shubman," I call out, my voice barely a whisper. He turns his head in my direction.

He stands straight, and I see his features clearly now. His eyes look droopy, and his expression is pained. My chest tightens at the sight, and I quickly close the gap between us. Before he can comprehend my presence, I wrap my arms around him tightly. His body tenses at first, but then, realizing it's me, he relaxes and holds me just as tightly. He buries his face in the crook of my neck, and I begin to rub his back soothingly.

When I try to break the hug, he clutches the fabric of my T-shirt, making a fist unwilling to let me go.

After standing like that for I dont know how long, I slowly pull back, running my fingers along his face, searching for any cuts or injuries. Shubman takes my hands, and brings them to his lips and kisses them gently,  before hugging me again. We stay like that, holding each other, finding solace in the embrace until -

*Thump thump*

A loud noise from outside the room startles us, breaking our hug. Despite his dizziness, Shubman instinctively steps in front of me, his tall frame shielding me. Before I can fully process what's happening, the door flings open.

For a moment, horror grips me. Then, as the figures come into focus, I realize they're cops. My shoulders relax with the understanding.

Someone flicks on the lights, illuminating the room. I see the detectives entering with Rohit, Virat, and Hardik Bhaiya.

Their demeanor relaxes when they see an unharmed Shubman standing there, but their expressions turn abashed when their eyes land on me. I brace myself for their wrath.

Rohit Bhaiya is the first to step forward. He hugs Shubman and ruffles his hair, trying to lighten the mood. "You know how tense I was, thinking about who I'd pick as my opening partner," he says, and Shubman lets out a low chuckle.

Then Virat Bhaiya embraces him, his voice stern as he threatens Shubman never to end up in a situation like this again.

Hardik Bhaiya follows, kissing Shubman on the cheek and patting his back while giving him a hug

All of them, though, shoot daggers at me with their eyes. As we start to leave the room, Shubman suddenly passes out. Panic grips us all, but the detective quickly reassures us. "It's okay, no need to panic. This happens a lot in situations like these. They must have given him something. We're going to take him to the hospital to examine if there's any kind of drug in his system."

When we finally step out of the bungalow, a chill runs down my spine. We see a plethora of police officers and detectives making arrests, it’s terrifying, truly.

I get into the ambulance with Virat Bhaiya, while Rohit and Hardik Bhaiya follow us.
