"Ishan bhai, shubman bhai ko kya hua hai?" Rinku asked Ishan while giving Shubman a look, who was sitting just two seats away from them. In response to this strange question, Ishan turned his head to look at what exactly was wrong with Shubman, but just as he turned his head, he came eye to eye with a smiling Shubman. The first instinct of Ishan was to look away, but he endured it and maintained eye contact instead.

The more he looked at Shubman, the wider his smile got, and he was forced to look away at the end. Rinku was so confused that he turned to Rishabh for answers, who just smiled at him. "Are you all good?" Rishabh asked Ishan as he took a seat beside him. "Ha?" "kahan ghum hai?" There was a different kind of glint in Rishabh's eyes, and it scared Ishan a bit, "Kuch nahi."

"Shubman, Sun Teko Rohit Bhai bulare hai." Just then Siraj came from in front of them and asked for Shubman. "Shubman." He on the other hand, was too busy staring at 'someone'. "SHUBMAN." Siraj lost his last shred of patience, and his yell finally woke Shubman up from his la-la land. "kya hua bhai?" "Kitna ghurega tu usko? Bas kar, ab chal, Rohit bhai bula rahe hain." And they left, leaving behind a hooting crowd for Ishan to handle.

"Something Something?" asked Rishabh, winking at Ishan. He felt like smacking the stupidity out of him, but he put his attention on the current strategy instead. It had been too long since Shubman had disappeared with Siraj, and Ishan was now starting to worry. His problem? He couldn't openly show his concern, giving them another reason to hoot, so he kept his concerns to himself.

Later, Shubman returned to the field with other members. "Shubman will play the mock match tomorrow," Rohit informed the team, making an excited clatter go about. They were all more excited to welcome Shubman back on the team than Shubman himself was. Ishan couldn't help himself but give a bright smile to Rohit.

Shubman faked losing consciousness and fell sideways over Virat when he saw that smile. "Bachche pagal ho gaya hain?" Virat scolded him half-heartedly. "Wo Ishaan ki smile dekhke thoda behosh ho gaya, bhai, samjho na." Ishan glared at Rishabh and shook his head, grabbing his kit to leave the group as soon as possible. "Sachai se koi kab tak hi bhage ga." Shubman shouted behind his retreating figure, to which Ishaan replied with a middle finger.

The next day came by a bit too early for Ishan's liking. Now they had a mock match, and he wasn't ready for it at all. After getting ready he looked at Shubman who was busy staring at the strings of his shoes with too much passion. "What are you doing?" Startled by Ishan's sudden question, Shubman started staring at Ishan instead.

Ishan, on the other hand, just smiled at Shubman and bent down to tie his shoelace for him, stunning him. "No, I'll do it." "Don't be nervous. You'll do great. Trust me." Ishan said, completely ignoring Shubman's request to let go of the shoelace.

"Do you trust me?" Ishan asked once more when he got no response. "More than I trust myself." The intensity of that sentence made Ishan lose his footing for a bit. He just gave Shubman a small smile and left the room, leaving all his things behind. Shubman just smiled to himself before grabbing Ishan's and his kit to go downstairs to the team.

"Oho, Ishan, princess treatment, ha." "Shut up, Rishabh. You didn't have to bring it, Shubman. I could've brought it down myself." Ishan told Shubman while taking his kit from him. "Yeah, but you ran, leaving it behind. What else was I supposed to do?" "Leave it just how it was. What else?" Ishan gave the sass before walking behind Rohit. Rishabh and Shubman looked at each other and smiled fondly.

Everybody liked Ishan a bit too much for Shubman's liking. He was new to this feeling and couldn't help but wonder just how strong the bond of this team was. If it were to be the old Shubman, he would never feel bad about Ishan interacting with the members like this. That is because he knew all of them so well. To this Shubman it wasn't a pretty sight at all.

Later when the match started, Shubman had this sudden urge to prove himself. Earlier, he wanted to do well and make the best of this opportunity, but now he had this weird urge to do the best. To make Ishan proud. With the single thought of Ishan going through his mind, Shubman entered the field.

'let's do it,' was what he told himself before he went for a six on the very first ball. It was a bold decision, but Shubman was never the one to back out from a challenge. Soon the first six followed a second and a third, and suddenly Shubman was nearing his half-century. He was opening with Rohit, and Ishan was at one down. Right now, he was sitting in the dugout with the rest of the team.

Every time Shubman hit a six, he instinctively searched for Ishan in the crowd. It felt completely natural to him, and Shubman had no complaints. Soon, he reached the half-century mark, celebrating his first milestone after the accident. Once again, he scanned the stands for Ishan and felt a warm rush when he saw him looking like the happiest person in the world at that moment.

He was smiling too wide and was clapping enthusiastically. He gave him a smile when their eyes met, and Shubman felt like leaving everything he was doing behind and running towards him. 'Jaake bhi tu na jaye, thehri tu dil mein haye, hasrat si banke kyun bhala.' The wicketkeeper from behind the stumps started singing, suddenly forcing Shubman to jolt awake. Shubman gave him a look in response to which he just smiled like nothing happened.

Shubman had just smashed 79 runs off 33 balls, and he was too close to the century he deserved to back off now. His conscience urged him to play slowly and cautiously, but his hands weren't ready to cooperate. He glanced over at Rohit, hoping for support, but Rohit simply shook his head, a look of affection on his face, leaving Shubman to find his rhythm.

As he approached the century, Shubman's heart began to race. He felt a mix of anxiety, happiness, and nervousness all at once. With 98 runs on the board... he was out. Shubman aimed for a single, but he was stumped before he could reach the crease. It all happened too quickly for him to comprehend, and just like that, his first century after the accident remained elusive once again.

As he returned to the dugout, he was too upset to even look up. He felt that things could have been better if he had just opened his eyes. The disappointment was evident on Rohit's face, and Shubman was too scared to glance at Ishan. Just as he was about to enter the dugout, two strong arms wrapped around him in a firm but warm hug.

'The scent,' Shubman realized—it was Ishan. He hugged him back as tightly as he could, feeling as if one of their ribs might break if the embrace continued for much longer. But Shubman didn't want to let go; he longed to hold onto the moment forever. Eventually, he had to release the hug, meeting Ishan's gaze as he did so.

Ishan simply smiled at Shubman and mouthed, 'I am proud of you.' Shubman felt like his heart might burst from overwhelming emotions; he felt completely fulfilled as if he had just won the world. Suddenly, a distant yet vivid memory flashed through his mind—a cricket ground where he was surrounded by a group of people. He remembered a boy smiling at him, and it evoked a warm happiness that made him feel like he could die content.

"Shubi," Ishan's concerned voice pulled him back from his thoughts. After giving Shubman a firm squeeze on the shoulder, Ishan went to take his turn. Shubman settled into the dugout, realizing that watching Ishan play wasn't a bad option after all. He thought to himself, 'If for some reason I couldn't play in the future, watching Ishan would be therapy enough.'

Ishan, after completing his half-century, gave Shubman a smile from the ground, and Shubman felt like he could melt on the spot. "itna mat hans bhai, face muscles dukhenge." Rishabh came from behind him when he saw him sitting there smiling like an idiot. "Chup re." "Aha, ye acha hai, chup re to anyone who is not Ishan, right?"

Shubman didn't deny it; he loved listening to Ishan. Perhaps it was because he had always enjoyed it, or maybe it was because he wanted to learn more about him now that he had lost his memories. Regardless, he appreciated it nonetheless. Soon, Ishan came out after scoring a solid 62 runs and joined the team in the dugout.

And so, Shubman returned to his usual habit of staring. Ishan, exasperated, shot him a glare and said, "You do realize there are cameras here, right?" This sudden remark made Shubman look around, while Rishabh tsked in response, "As if he's ever cared about the cameras before."

Virat, sitting beside them, let out a hearty laugh. "Well, Ishi baby, he has always been staring at you; the whole world knows, hahaha!"

Ishan turned to Virat in stunned silence before glancing back at the crowd. It was true that Shubman was often the one initiating these stares, but at that moment, he hadn't confessed his feelings yet. This made it seem like they were just friendly glances, but now it felt different, and it scared Ishan.
