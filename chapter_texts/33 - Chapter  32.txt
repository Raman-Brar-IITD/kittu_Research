•ISHAN KISHAN•

In my room, I find myself glancing at the door every five minutes, hoping that Shubman might burst in unexpectedly, and tell me some wild story about how he got stuck with elephants or something. I can almost hear his voice, filled with excitement and laughter, before he pulls me into a tight hug and whispers that he missed me.

I gaze at the empty space beside me, he always insisted on piling two pillows under his head, even though it often meant waking up with a sore neck. The sight of the vacant spot tugs at my heart, a reminder of his absence.

I still remember the time when he made me question my sexuality- I was straight, wasn't I? But when he came into my life as my fake boyfriend, everything I thought I knew about myself began to change.

(Reference - When Ishan was heading to Shubman's house for a Pooja, he got into an accident. Ishan wasn't hurt, but the other person was hospitalized)

I still remember the time when he held me close on the bed, my tears soaking his t-shirt, I was a crying mess but he provided me the warmth of his embrace, the way he whispered soothing words, remains etched in my memory. I can still feel the tenderness of his lips when he planted a gentle kiss on my forehead, that spoke volumes of his affection and care.

___

I recall the conversation with Mayank vividly.

"What's going on between you two?" he asked, his eyes narrowing with curiosity.

"Between who?" I replied, feigning ignorance.

"You and Shubman," he said pointedly.

"Nothing," I insisted, but my voice lacked conviction.

"Come on, Ishan," Mayank pressed. "Didn't you see how jealous he got when you mentioned that I'm your best friend? And when you were treating my cut?"

I had noticed. I noticed everything-- the subtle shifts in Shubman's demeanor, the flash of jealousy in his eyes. Yet a part of me kept insisting that I was imagining things, seeing something that wasn't really there.

I remember the times when he would glance at me from across the room,

how his face fell when I mentioned I was going out with Mayank, the disappointment written all over him.

When he called me to come back, stating some silly reasons. I noticed everything. It was right in front of me. How could I not?

When I returned and found him asleep on the couch, my heart melted at the sight. He looked so utterly endearing. He is silly, he is goofy, and sometimes he makes me question my own existence. But he is also beautiful, not just on the outside, but deep within.

He has a way of making me smile with his antics, always putting in the effort to make me happy. He hugs me when I need it the most, and he makes breakfast for me, even though he whines incessantly when I'm not around or I give attention to someone who is not him.

In his own unique, heartfelt way, he shows me just how much he cares.

He was asleep, and a voice inside me screamed, "Kiss him-- he'll never know."

Oh, how I liked the idea. Of course I did. Hadn't I wanted to do that for so long, no matter how much I told myself that I didn't? But I did. Maybe a bit too much.

I leaned over him, my heart racing frantically. The idea of my lips touching his filled me with a mix of excitement and nervousness. But before I could do anything, his eyes fluttered open, and I jerked back, standing up and fumbling with my words. "Uh, umm, I was waking you, but you pulled my hand in your sleep, so--" I stammered.

He didn't notice my awkwardness, groggy as he was, and I thanked all the heavens for it. My mind was crowded with all kinds of questions. What if he had realized what I was trying to do? What would he think?

When we finally lay on the bed that day, his hand accidentally brushed against mine under the covers. Neither of us moved. We left our hands where they were, connected by that simple, but electrifying touch.

Every morning, I always found myself in his embrace, waking up to the warmth and safety of his arms around me. But in that moment, the touch of our hands under the covers meant more than any embrace. It wasn't just him wanting to feel my touch, it was me, too, longing for his.

___

(Refrence - when they were going to crash Ellisa's wedding)

I helped him put on the jacket, and after doing so, I ran my hand over the lapels, a soft smile tugging at my lips. I took a few steps back to look at him properly and, with a smile playing on my lips, remarked casually, "Not bad."

"Just 'not bad'?" he countered. "You were practically speechless yesterday when you laid eyes on me in this." I agreed with him, but I wasn't going to admit it.

"Don't flatter yourself," I retorted. "I'm not going to say that you're looking utterly charming and have the capability to pull anyone you want right now."

He took a step towards me. "Hmm, did you realize you just praised me?"

Oh, I absolutely did praise him. "No, I didn't," I said, feigning innocence.

"You did," he said, a wide grin spreading across his face. He looked so happy, as if my praise had sent him on cloud nine.

Placing both my hands gently against his chest and applying slight pressure, I admitted, "Okay, okay, you look stunning right now. I might just find myself falling for you."

Raising his eyebrow, he asked teasingly, "You might?"

'Oh, May be I already am,' -- my inner voice spoke.

__

"Wait a minute," he said, approaching the cupboard and taking out a shopping bag, handing it to me. I was confused as to why he was giving me anything, but when I opened it, I realized it was the same shirt he had asked me to buy.

"This shirt-" I began, my gaze shifting between him and the clothing in my hands.

"Yes, I bought it for you," he confessed with a mischievous grin. Did my heart melt a little? No, it melted a lot because it's not about the shirt; it's about his efforts and these small gestures.

"You didn't have to," I protested for the sake of it.

"But I wanted to," he insisted. "Try it on. I have a feeling it will suit you perfectly."

When I emerged wearing the black satin shirt, the sight left him awestruck. I rolled one sleeve, and before I could move to do the other, he took the opportunity. With a gentle touch, he held the other sleeve and started rolling it up. I let him do it without any protest.

He fixed my gold chain. "My choice is really good," he said, not looking at the shirt but looking into my eyes. The intensity in his eyes made me blush, making me want to hide my face in my palms-or perhaps in his chest.

"I mean the shirt, it's my choice, right?" he said teasingly.

"Of course, you mean the shirt," I responded defensively.

___

“Oh really? Then prove it,” the bridesmaid said, her tone was challenging.

“How?”

“I don’t know, do something… kiss him.” As soon as the words left her mouth, my demeanor momentarily cracked. But hadn’t I wanted to do that for the longest time? Kiss him? Feel his lips against mine? And wasn’t this the perfect time, an excuse handed to me on a silver platter? I couldn’t let this chance slip away. Could I?

I barely registered Shubman saying, “Listen--” before he could complete the sentence I was on my feet.  I seized the lapel of his tuxedo and pulled him towards me, pressing my lips against his.

In that instant, all the uncertainty, confusion, and fear I had melted away. The feel of his lips on mine was surreal, more perfect than I had ever imagined. I wanted to move my lips, to deepen the kiss, but before I could, he moved his, and nothing could compare to that moment. People always talked about butterflies in their stomachs, but in that moment, I felt an entire zoo. How could a mere kiss feel so blissful?

I felt his hand snake around my waist, pulling me closer. I started to reciprocate, losing myself in the kiss. In that moment, there was nothing else-- It was just me, him, and the kiss.

___

(Reference - Shubman's confession)

"I want to say something, or more like confess something," he spoke hesitantly, his voice barely above a whisper. He waited for my reaction, but I kept pretending to be asleep.

When he was sure I was asleep, he began to pour out his heart. As he spoke, confessing his feelings for me, all I could think of 'didn't? didn't I feel the same?'

He continued, his voice was filled with vulnerability, "Sometimes, I’m afraid you can read my eyes and see what I feel for you,"  Yes, I could read it in his eyes. They were never subtle - the gleam in them, the stolen glances, the way they softened, especially when they gazed at mine.

All the while, he caressed my cheek with his thumb, his touch gentle and soothing. My chest rose and fell steadily, but inside, I struggled to maintain my calm facade.

His confession caught me off guard, and while he believed I was asleep, I absorbed every word, every emotion. He planted a soft kiss on my forehead, sending a shiver down my spine. "Ishan, I lo--" he hesitated, his voice trembling. "I really like you, Ishan," he finally said.

I should have kept my eyes shut, but the overwhelming need to see his face at that moment compelled me to open them slowly. When I did, I saw his eyes quickly close, but not before I caught the panic in them.

Finally, I knew his true feelings, and he had no idea that I shared them completely.

A few moments passed, and then I leaned in, planting a soft, gentle kiss on his forehead while caressing his cheek just as he had done while confessing. I hoped to relay the message that I completely reciprocated his feelings, but he was too dumb to understand anything.

"In the clear footage, Mr. Gill wasn't willing to get into the car. He was forced to... They were kidnapping him."

My heart sinks.
