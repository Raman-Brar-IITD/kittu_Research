"You look so deep in thought these days; what is wrong, Shubi?" Ishan asked one night as they sat on the balcony of Shubman's room in Chandigarh. Shubman and Ishaan, along with Shubman's family, had recently returned to their hometown. Ishan had stayed with Shubman while in Mumbai, and he went with them when they proposed to stay at home for some time before either of them had to go back to cricket.

"nothing." Shubman had replied, but he didn't look back at Ishan. He kept looking in the distance, thinking about something. It had become too common for Shubman these past few weeks to zone out. Although it was not uncommon or unusual, its appearance had become too frequent lately.

Shubman would look at you and talk to you, but he wouldn't be fully into the conversation. It was as if something was so awake in his mind that he kept drifting off from reality sometimes. Everyone had noticed it. They were all concerned about Shubman, of course, but they also knew that coming back from the state that he had been in was not an easy task and would definitely have some side effects on Shubman's conscious.

So, they had tried to ignore it as much as possible, but now they couldn't ignore it anymore. So, Ishan had decided to break the ice. "Shubi," he said softly, scooting closer, his arm wrapping around Shubman's shoulder. "If something is bothering you, please tell me." Shubman leaned into him but said nothing. Ishan didn't push. He just stayed—warm, steady, present.

Ten minutes passed in silence. Then, without warning, Shubman turned and pulled Ishan into a bone-crushing hug. "I love you," he whispered into Ishan's neck, his voice trembling.

Ishan just smiled into his shoulder and hugged back tighter. Then suddenly, as if he realized something, Ishan's smile dropped for a second, and he pulled back from the hug, cradling Shubman's face in his palm. "You've been overthinking this, haven't you? this whole time?" And Shubman remained silent. "Baby, look at me," Ishan said gently, tilting his face until their eyes met. "You have to talk to me. I can't help you if you shut me out."

And finally, after days of silence, Shubman met his gaze fully. Their eyes locked, and for a heartbeat, the world stilled. Shubman's eyes were glassy, swimming with unsaid words. He opened his mouth to say something, but nothing came out. Shubman had been thinking about his future with Ishan and about that fateful confession at the hospital.

Shubman had loved Ishan for the majority of the time that he had spent with him. Now that he finally had his Ishan with him, the thoughts of family and people finally decided to make an appearance. It was not like Shubman hadn't thought about all this before, but back then he saw no possible chance of them coming together. He didn't think, even in his wildest of dreams, that Ishan would ever reciprocate his feelings. And so, the thoughts of what people might think had always been secondary in Shubman's mind.

It was not the same anymore. Now, Shubman had Ishan to himself, and nothing else mattered for some time before he realized that he had two families to confess his sexuality to and tons of friends who didn't know about him yet. He had forgotten that he was a public figure in a country where people valued not just your game but also your moral character and everything that is not in relation to the sport you play at all.

He forgot that he was a cricketer in a country that worshipped cricket and wouldn't let anyone live that taints it even by a bit. Shubman had always thought that these things didn't affect him at all, that he wouldn't give two fucks about people if they had a problem with him and his sexuality. But it wasn't the same anymore.

Shubman wasn't at his prime anymore. He had just gotten back from an accident that could have very well taken his life. He was vulnerable and weak. And he wasn't alone. When he had thought about people knowing his sexuality and judging him it was just him that was going to be judged and bashed because he was the only one in the picture.

Now, though, there was also Ishan. He would die before he would let anything touch Ishan and his character. He had never imagined a possibility where Ishan would be his, and therefore he never thought of the scenario where he would need to be dragged into Shubman's drama. But now that Ishan was also a part of this, it was scarier than thought itself.

There were butterflies in his stomach every time he thought about Ishan reciprocating his feelings, but now there was just anxiety rolling in his veins. He wished to be as brave as his forgotten self. He wished that he didn't use his brain at all. But unfortunately for him, that was not how things worked. He had to be present, and he had to think things through. He had to be brave, but he also had to be strategic.

"Shubman, say it," Ishan demanded, and Shubman broke. "I—Ishi, I don't think that it is as easy as it looks. I can't endanger your career." And Ishan frowned at that before a majestic smile appeared on his angelic face. Shubman looked at Ishan, who sat there trying to stifle his laughter.

"Are you the same person from before? Because honestly, Shubman, you didn't seem very concerned about my career when you decided that it was a good time in the middle of the practice field around all the other players present there to ask me, 'Have I always been romantically into you, or have I started losing my mind behind you now?'"

That one sentence brought back the memories that Shubman desperately tried to forget. That made all the blood in his body rush to his head and make him a bit dizzy. Shubman groaned, burying his face in his hands. "Can we not talk about that?" he asked in a small voice, almost wishing that the void would swallow him right that moment.

"No, we absolutely are talking about that," Ishan said, laughing. "You confessed your undying love for me in the middle of a crowded field like it was a Shah Rukh Khan movie. Now you want to play shy?" It was as if this was the most ridiculous thing that he had heard all his life. "I was not in my right mind then." "Yeah, you tell me about that."

"Can you please stop with the teasing?" Shubman asked, burying his face in Ishan's shoulder while Ishan laughed harder. "No, because weren't you, like, 'courting me,' Shubi? You didn't seem very concerned when you told me how you didn't care about anyone else liking me because you were the one courting me right that moment." And Shubman pinched Ishan's upper arm to stop him from speaking anymore.

"Ow! You menace," Ishan laughed. "You've been walking around like someone died because you're 'concerned' about me? Be serious, Shubman."

"I am serious," Shubman said, finally pulling away, voice firm but vulnerable. "It might not seem like a big deal to you, but I am concerned. What if people react badly? What if they bash you for being with me? I'm not at my prime anymore, Ishi. I'm barely even back. I don't want you to pay for my truth."

"And please don't even get me started about how I need to convince both our families. I am scared of your brother." Ishan's face softened at that. He leaned in and pressed their foreheads together. "Shubman Gill," he whispered, "if you think our families—or anyone who truly matters—aren't already aware of how in love you are with me, then you need professional help for that obliviousness."

Shubman huffed out a soft laugh despite himself. "They might be a little surprised if I say I don't love you romantically, but if you try and convince them it's platonic, they'll just laugh in your face. So, no, you don't need to worry about 'convincing' anyone. And about anyone that doesn't accept us, we don't need to prove anything to anyone; if they don't want to accept us, they have a choice to not accept us."

Shubman looked down, his voice quieter. "But what about everyone else? The fans, the press, the people who'll twist everything we do into something ugly?" "We can't live our lives for everyone else," Ishan said, brushing a thumb over his cheek.

"We'll never win that battle. People judge. That's what they do. But we—we only need each other. Everyone who matters already supports us." He paused, then leaned closer. "Trust me." And this time, Shubman did.

He looked at Ishan like he was seeing the sun after a long, bitter winter. All the noise in his head—the spiraling doubts, the what-ifs, the imagined failures—quieted in that moment. It was just Ishan. Always, Ishan.

Shubman cupped the side of Ishan's face, his thumb gently tracing the edge of his jaw as if memorizing it with touch alone. There was a slight tremble in his hand, the vulnerability of a man who had fought too long and too quietly. His voice came out low, thick with emotion. "I think I was waiting for someone to tell me it was okay to stop being scared."

Ishan's eyes softened. His hand moved up to rest over Shubman's, fingers brushing tenderly against his knuckles. "It's okay," he whispered, his voice low and warm like honey on a winter morning. "It's okay to stop being scared now."

And then it happened.

Their lips met—not rushed, not uncertain, but with the kind of quiet intensity that comes from longing held back too long. Shubman kissed him like he had something to prove—not to Ishan, but to himself. That this was real. That Ishan was his, here, now. It wasn't tentative; it was deliberate. Deep. Unfolding slowly, like a story they'd waited too long to tell.

Ishan responded in kind, lips parting slightly as he pulled Shubman closer by the waist, feeling his heartbeat thunder through the layers between them. His fingers slipped into Shubman's curls, anchoring him, grounding him. The kiss deepened, slow and all-consuming, the kind that left no room for doubt. There was no performance here, no expectations—just two hearts speaking in a language that didn't need words.

The world around them blurred. The soft rustling of leaves, and the hum of distant traffic—all faded into the background. There was only the warmth of Ishan's mouth, the tenderness in his hands, and the grounding rhythm of their shared breaths. The softness of Ishan's lips made Shubman's heart beat a little faster than it already was. This was like a distant dream to Shubman. A dream that he was scared to even dream about subconsciously. A dream that he never thought would feel so vivid, so real.

When they finally pulled apart, foreheads resting against each other, Shubman's eyes were glassy but calm. His breathing was unsteady, his lips a little swollen, but there was something new in his expression—something like peace. Like coming home after being lost too long.

Ishan pressed a gentle kiss to his forehead, letting it linger. "You're mine, Shubi," he said softly, his voice more certain than it had ever been. "And I'm yours. We'll figure the rest out together."

Shubman closed his eyes at that, letting the words settle over him like a blanket. The fear hadn't vanished completely—but it no longer ruled him. Not when Ishan looked at him like this. Not when Ishan kissed him like he was worth it.

And at that moment, wrapped in each other on the quiet balcony under the patient stars, Shubman felt something he hadn't felt in what seemed like a lifetime—he felt safe. Seen. Whole. He wasn't walking this path alone anymore. And finally, he allowed himself to believe that love like this could be enough.
