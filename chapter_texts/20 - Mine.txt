The silence in the corridor was palpable. They could all sense it; there was a shift in the air. Ishan looked like he was high on something, and they all looked at Hardik with curious eyes. Ishan was ever a charmer; he was anything but nervous. He was the sanctuary most people looked up to when under pressure.

The always bubbly, ever calm, the edge of serenity Ishan Kishan looked nervous. Or was he scared? Was it a concern on his face? They couldn't figure it out no matter how hard they tried, and they certainly couldn't ask him because that was definitely out of the question at the moment.

There were thousands of emotions swarming in his eyes, yet they couldn't pinpoint even one of them. They had expected Ishan to burst in here and yell at them for not informing him when Shubman woke up; they expected Ishan to act out of character, but this was something else.

The forced serenity and the uncomfortable calm were fucking with their brains; they needed Ishan to react, to shout and scream, or even to throw a little smile here or crack a joke there, but Ishan stood there near Shubman's door like his life was on the line. The emotions in his eyes turned off momentarily when the doctors left the room.

The female doctor who had taken it upon herself to make them as comfortable as possible walked towards them and smiled at Ishan. "They must have already informed you what was wrong, didn't they?" And before Ishan could conjure up words to answer her, she continued. It was almost as if she could sense the tension too.

"It is now all okay. Shubman had completely regained his memories without any complications, and you can meet him and talk to him like you would any other day without panicking." Then she smiled at Shubman's mother and left them to their elements.

They contemplated for a moment before collectively ignoring the eyes with which Ishan looked at Hardik. What they couldn't ignore was the shrug of Hardik's shoulder before he entered the room. Without any hesitation, without so much as a second thought. And behind him entered the entire crowd of people, with Ishan being the last of them all.

"Aur bhai, sab thik?" Hardik slapped Shubman's leg the moment he entered the room, and that earned him a slap on the arm by Virat, and the mood got lifted again, the awkward silence effectively ignored. "So, you remember everything now?" Rishabh asked stupidly. This earned him a lot of questioning glares.

"Yes, Rishabh, I do. You still doubt that?" Shubman asked, smiling at him, but then that smile suddenly got replaced by a frown the moment Shahneel walked a little further, pushing Hardik to one side and revealing the existence of Ishan. Shubman met Ishan's eyes, and the tension in the room felt suffocating.

Ishan looked like he wanted to say something, but all he did was smile at Shubman a bit too sweetly, while Shubman looked like he could combust on the spot at any moment. Shubman's cheeks were a brilliant shade of red, and he looked at Virat with eyes that had signs of SOS printed in them.

"I think I forgot to switch the AC off back at home, god the bill." Shubman's mother said and tried to leave in an extreme hurry while dragging her half-clueless husband with her. Shahneel looked like she had been holding her laugh for quite some time now, and like the good person she was, she decided to not laugh in her brother's face and embarrass him even more, so she too quietly exited while muttering a work excuse.

Virat took one look at Hardik's stupid smile and decided that that was it. He grabbed Hardik by the collar of his shirt and muttered something that suspiciously sounded like a curse before dragging him out of the room with himself. Rishabh stood there rooted on the spot, looking around like people had suddenly grown horns.

That was before Abhishek kicked him out the door before following him and closing the door behind them. Ishan and Shubman were very much aware of what was happening around them, and the last stunt that Abhishek had pulled, not even bothering to mask it for any sort of reason, had brought out a hollering laughter from Ishan, while Shubman just sat there looking mortified and at a complete loss for words.

As the door clicked shut behind Abhishek, a hush settled once again, softer this time, like the calm that follows a storm everyone had secretly been waiting for. Ishan's laughter trickled into a breath, and his gaze found Shubman's again.

Shubman, poor thing, looked like he'd been caught cheating in an exam — flushed, fidgeting, and very obviously regretting every bold decision his amnesia had encouraged him to make. "You," Ishan said, finally walking closer, arms folded, "look like you'd rather be facing a bouncer from Mitchell Starc than be here with me right now."

Shubman groaned, dragging a hand across his face. "That's because I would rather be doing that." Ishan let out a soft laugh again, but it didn't quite reach his eyes. He stopped by the edge of the hospital bed and looked at Shubman for a beat too long until the younger man fidgeted again.

"Tell me something," Ishan began, his voice gentle now, "do you remember what you said to me? Before... before everything came back?" Shubman looked like someone had turned gravity up on him. He straightened slightly, his eyes darting to Ishan's and then to the blanket he was now pretending to be intensely interested in. "I... I said a lot of things, I think."

"Right," Ishan nodded, stepping even closer now, voice steady. "But I'm asking about that thing. The one that made me forget how to breathe for a good ten seconds." Shubman sucked in a breath. "Ishan, I didn't— I mean, I wasn't thinking clearly. My head was all messed up, and maybe I said too much too fast—"

"Shubman," Ishan cut him off, and his tone made the younger man shut up instantly. "Don't do that. Don't backtrack now." There was silence. Ishan's voice was softer when he spoke again. "You forgot me, and you still found your way back. You didn't even remember who I was, and yet somehow... you still looked at me like I hung the damn stars in the sky."

Shubman's lips parted, stunned. "You don't get to do that, confess like that, and then take it back just because your memories rebooted. That's not how this works." Shubman blinked rapidly. "So what are you saying?"

Ishan looked away for a moment, then back at him, eyes glossy. "I'm saying..." He huffed, clearly frustrated at his own lack of words. "I'm saying I think I completely understand your feelings because maybe I felt it too, deep down. And maybe I was too dumb, or too scared, or too stubborn to see it for what it was. But when you forgot me and fell for me again—"

His voice broke a little, and he paused to swallow thickly. "—I realized you've always seen me in ways I've been too afraid to believe I deserve. And now all I can think about is how it took you forgetting me to remember us." Shubman looked like his entire operating system had shut down. He opened his mouth. Then closed it. Then opened it again. "Ishan..."

"No. Let me finish," Ishan said, stepping closer until their knees nearly touched. "When you said those things to me before your memories came back, you didn't sound confused, Shubman. You sounded free. Like, for once, your heart wasn't fighting your brain." Shubman's eyes were welling up now.

"Idiot," Ishan whispered, smiling through the tears threatening to spill. "You're my idiot. The annoying, batting-stance-obsessed, second-chance-taking idiot who apparently has to forget me just to admit he likes me." Shubman huffed a laugh, and a single tear ran down his cheek. "You're unbelievable."

"And you're slow," Ishan teased, gently wiping the tear with his thumb. "But you're also mine. If you still want to be." Shubman didn't respond with words. He leaned forward, shaky but sure, pressing their foreheads together, and whispered, "I wanted to be yours even when I didn't remember your name."

Ishan finally let out a teary chuckle. "You're such a sap." "Takes one to love one," Shubman murmured, and Ishan laughed again, the kind of laugh that cracked him open and healed him all at once.

Outside the door, a few very nosy teammates fist-bumped each other silently. And inside, in the sterile quiet of a hospital room, two people began something that had always been written for them — memory or not.
