When Ishan woke from the blissful slumber, the first thing he noticed was someone's arm protectively draped over him. He was being held so close, it almost hurt—whether from the grip of the person holding him or the aching swell in his chest, he wasn't sure. Maybe it was the overwhelming urge to cry when he realized it was Shubman holding him.

Anchored. In place.

He wanted to look up—to see Shubman's peaceful face, to make sure he wasn't hallucinating. But he also wanted to stay right where he was, in Shubman's arms, forever. He lingered in that dilemma for a while until a voice broke the silence—a sweet, velvety voice that could convince him to commit the most horrendous crimes, and he'd do them all in a trance.

"For how long are you going to pretend to be asleep?" Shubman asked with a soft chuckle. A chuckle that might've given Ishan a heart attack had his heart not already been somewhere else entirely. Ishan looked up, to see that stupidly perfect smile tugging at Shubman's lips. Abruptly, he sat up. "You're dangerous for my heart, Shubi."

Shubman thought that was utterly ridiculous—everyone knew exactly who was dangerous for whom—but he smiled anyway. "You scared me. What happened earlier?" he asked, resting his head on Ishan's shoulder. At that question, the memories hit Ishan like a hurricane.

"Shubman," his voice cracked. Ishan turned to face him, taking his hand in both of his own. "Shubi..." It was hard to speak. Shubman was looking at him like an obedient puppy, eyes soft and open, the eyes he loved so much. "I'm so sorry," Ishan whispered. It was all too much.

"Shhh, baby... You don't have anything to be sorry for." Shubman pulled him into another hug, and Ishan broke. Again. He was sobbing like he'd lost everything—and maybe he had. Maybe everything that was now finally within his grasp had once been so far out of reach, that it had broken him.

"No, Shubi. It was my fault. I shouldn't have pushed you away. I shouldn't have avoided the confrontation. I thought I was keeping you safe. I thought I was protecting our friendship. But—fuck—I don't know why I ever believed I could keep anything safe at all."   "Shh. Stop speaking nonsense, Ishu. You are my safe place. I feel safest when I'm with you. If you can't keep something safe, then no one can. Okay?"

"Look at you—still trying to cheer me up when I almost got you killed." Ishan's body shivered at the word killed, and it shattered something inside Shubman. "No, Ishan. Look at me." His voice held a quiet command that Ishan couldn't disobey. He lifted his gaze.

"You are the love of my life. Do you understand that, Ishan Kishan? If I had the chance to go back and redo everything, I'd still choose to save you. Do you know why? Because I love you. I have always loved you. Even before I knew what love meant, you were the one I loved the most."

"You avoided me because you were trying to protect us—what we had. You were doing the right thing in your own way. But let me tell you something: I could die for you, Ishu. But don't ever ask me to stay away from you. That would kill me more than death ever could." Ishan placed a hand over Shubman's mouth then, trying to stop him from speaking any more about death.

"Let me finish." Shubman gently moved Ishan's hand away. "I was mad at you. Yes. But not because you owe me anything—not because you're obligated to talk to me. I was mad because you decided what was best for me without asking me. Because you, Ishan Kishan, decided that being away from me was the best thing for me."

"That is the most brilliantly stupid thing in the world because even a stranger would know—you being away from me for even a second takes a year off my life."

"You, pretty little human, are the bane of my existence. I'd rather learn to live without oxygen than try to live without you. And none of this—none of what happened—was your fault. I jumped in for you, and I would unapologetically do it a thousand times over. You breathing, you being happy—that's all I need. With me or without me."

If Ishan was crying like a toddler throwing a tantrum, well—that was between him, Shubman, and the quiet safety of this hospital room. How had he been so lucky? How had he ever managed to deserve Shubman?

"Repeat after me: I—" Ishan looked at him like he'd grown horns but still repeated. Because when had he ever denied Shubman anything? "I." "Am. Not. Responsible." "I am not responsible." Ishan echoed, voice already cracking. "For your accident." "For your accident." His voice nearly broke on the last word.

"You hear that? You are not responsible for anything that happened to me. You're not responsible for me being in love with you. You're not responsible for me being stupid. And you are definitely not responsible for my accident. Do you understand?" Ishan could only nod. Because words—what even were they?
