•SHUBMAN GILL•

The room feels smaller with each step I take, the gravity of the situation drawing upon me. Yesterday, I approached the whole situation with nonchalance, not considering any consequences. But today, the weight of our plan presses down upon me, and I pace the room, feeling anxious.

On the other hand, Ishan looks quite calm, or at least he seems so, his demeanor not betraying any hint of the anxiety that I am feeling right now.

"Ishan," I begin, my voice betraying the tremor of uncertainty that is gripping me, "I'm actually scared."

He casts a sidelong glance in my direction and arches an eyebrow, asking, "Of ghosts?" he says, referencing last night's incident, a wry smile playing on his lips.

But I don't have time to get embarrassed over that. "No... I'm scared that we'll end up messing up the whole 'shaadi rukwao' thing."

A chuckle escapes Ishan's lips, though it lacks any real mirth. "What could possibly go wrong?" his words laced with confidence.

"I don't know, may be everything"

As the time is approaching, a weird sensation swirls inside me, probably anxiety, making me feel uneasy.

Ishan's movements disrupt the heavy silence of the room as he retrieves the wedding tuxedo from its resting place within the cupboard. As I take it, its weight feels heavy in my hand, It feels ominous.

I try to tie the bow but struggle with it. Ishan watches, looking as if he's waiting for me to call him for help. After seeing my struggle, he himself approaches towards me and takes matters into his own hands.

With precise movements, he starts to tie the bow. In the process, his cold fingers brush against my warm skin, sending a shiver down my spine and eliciting a physical response from me. Ishan either doesn't notice or pretends not to.

He ties the bow with perfection."Done," I turn around to look in the mirror, and the bow is perfect. "Where did you learn it?"

"YouTube tutorials."

"For me?"

"For who else?"

Okay, this one is smooth.

He helps me put on the jacket, and after doing so, he runs his hand over the lapels, a soft smile tugging at his lips. He takes a few steps back to look at me properly and  with a smile playing on his lips, "Not bad," he remarks casually.

"Just 'not bad'?" I counter. "You were practically speechless yesterday when you laid eyes on me in this." I tease him about his earlier speechlessness.

He playfully rolls his eyes, "Don't flatter yourself," he retorts with a grin. "I'm not going to say that you're looking utterly charming and have the capability to pull anyone you want right now."I don't think it was his intention, but somehow his words come out genuine.

Taking a deliberate step closer to him, I can't help but challenge his denial. "Hmm, did you realize you just praised me?"

A feigned look of innocence crosses his features as he takes a subtle step back. "No, I didn't," he insists, though I can see a flicker of amusement in his eyes.

"You did," I counter with a wide grin spreading across my face.

A hint of pink tints his cheeks as he attempts to divert the conversation, placing both hands gently against my chest and applying slight pressure. "Okay, okay, you look stunning right now. I might just find myself falling for you," he admits, his tone softening with a touch of vulnerability.

"You might?" I ask teasingly, raising my eyebrow.

The exchange between us begins as light-hearted banter, filled with playful jabs and teasing remarks. However, the atmosphere shifts subtly when I notice a faint blush creeping across Ishan's ears. "Your ears—" Before things could escalate, Ishan pushes me away gently, reminding me about our lateness. "We're going to be late."

"Huh ah-.......yes late" I stammer with my words a little as I realise how close we were and -

After calming a little I see Ishan taking a shirt out and I realise that the shirt I brought for him yesterday, I forget to give him. "Wait a minute" I say approaching the cupboard and taking out the shopping bag and giving it to him he was confused as why I am giving anything to him but when he opened it he realised that it's the same shirt that I was asking him to buy.

"This shirt ––" he begins, his gaze shifting between me and the clothing in his hands.

"Yes, I bought it for you," I confess with a mischievous grin,

"You didn't have to," he protest though a flicker of gratitude shine in his eyes.

"But I wanted to," I insist, my tone softening with sincerity. "Try it on. I have a feeling it will suit you perfectly."

When he emerges wearing that sleek black satin shirt, the sight leaves me awestruck. He rolls the sleeves of the shirt upwards, his hair is set with a few strands cascading effortlessly across his forehead.

He rolls one sleeve, and before he can move to do the other, I take the opportunity. With a gentle touch, I hold his other sleeve and roll it up, revealing his tattoos underneath. He lets me do it without saying anything. All this time, we hold each other's gaze.

I fix his gold chain. "My choice is really good," I declare with a satisfied smile. He looks toward me with a hint of amusement in his expression."I mean the shirt, it's my choice, right?" I say teasingly. He acts as if he knew I was talking about the shirt and not about anything else."Of course, you mean the shirt," he responds, his tone a little defensive but cute.

After getting ready, we all meet in Surya Bhai's room. He, Siraj Bhai, and Shreya are already there, where Surya Bhai himself is practically shining in his over-the-top shiny coat.

The mission? To sneak out of the hotel without attracting any unwanted attention. Easy, right? Well, not quite.

As luck would have it (or rather, not have it), we get caught on our way out by none other than Rohit and Virat Bhaiya. Virat Bhaiya gives me this long, perplexed look and says, "Why do you look like you're about to tie the knot?" I almost blurt out that I'm actually on my way to break up a wedding, but thankfully, I bite my tongue.

We didn't think about getting caught, so we don't have any good excuses either. All five of us start looking at each other for some believable answers when Shreyas, God bless his soul, comes up with a brilliant idea: "vo bhaiya, Shubman has a photoshoot, that's why he is wearing this."

"Huh, a photoshoot?" Virat Bhaiya raises his eyebrows, clearly skeptical.

"Yes, bhaiya," I say following Shreyas's script.

"But how did you get a shoot here? No one knew that we were going to stay here," Virat Bhaiya questions, grilling me about the logistics.

"Um, actually, I don't know how I got this shoot. Darshan called me and told me about it, so..." I trail off, hoping Virat Bhaiya is somewhat satisfied with my answer.

Then, the moment of glory arrives: "By the way, you are looking handsome" Jksksksksksksks Virat Bhaiya actually compliments me on my looks. I grin like a Cheshire cat – being praised by The Virat Kohli himself is a major achievement.

But just when we thought we were in the clear, Rohit Bhaiya has to  ruin it by asking, "You have your shoot, but why are these guys so ready as if they're going to a party?" He says pointing toward Ishan, Shreyas, Siraj, and Surya Bhai. Siraj Bhaiya's attempt to save the day by saying, "We are going to support Shubi," his futile attempt meets with raised eyebrows and incredulous stares.

"What?" Rohit Bhaiya raised his eyebrows, listening to the absurd lie Siraj Bhaiya just spat out.

Ishan leaned his head towards Siraj Bhaiya a little and with an awkward smile, he whispered, "Bhaiya, thoda believable jhoot bol le ?"

Surya Bhaiya, ever the smooth operator, tried to salvage the situation. He said, "I have a friend here, and he invited us for lunch."

Virat Bhaiya looked at his watch and said, "Lunch? At 3 pm?"

"Um, it was supposed to be lunch, but we are a little late. He'll still host us, don't worry," Shreyas took the matter into his hands.

"Why would I worry?" scoffed Virat Bhaiya.

Virat and Rohit Bhaiya, not entirely convinced but apparently tired of our shenanigans, let us off the hook. And with that, we finally made our escape. Once outside, Surya Bhai couldn't contain his laughter at Siraj bhaiya's feeble attempt "Seriously, Siraj? 'We are going to support Shubi'... What a hilarious lie."

Shreyas chimed in too, reliving the moment when Rohit Bhaiya's expression was pure gold. "Did you see Rohit Bhai's face when he said that? I thought we were going to have to tell them the truth, but you saved our asses."

Surya Bhai did an imaginary hair flip.
