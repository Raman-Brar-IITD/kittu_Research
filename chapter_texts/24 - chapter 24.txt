Disappointed is not the word for what Shubman feels, it's more like grief, mourning for his chances to explain. He's bouncing back and forth between denial and sadness, with bouts of anger and heart-crushing moments of acceptance.

Pinning is one thing, he's been doing it for years it's almost like second nature, but not actively having Ishan in his life is horrible, and impossible too given that they practically share every moment.

Shubman knows what suffering feels like.He's feeling it every day when he wakes up and realises all over again that Ishan is gone after finding his side of the bed empty. When he gets ready for practice all by himself or when he spends the rest of his day cooped up by himself.

He feels it whenever he goes to sleep at night hugging a pillow, it's a habit Ishan left for him to deal with.

He knows what suffering feels like, Ishan made him feel it every day in his absence.

Homesickness that's what it is, he had dealt with it before and probably will again in future too. This time though it's harder than ever.

Shubman worries every time he sees the response he got to his text from Ishan, and they haven't talked after that shubman doesn't know how to respond or what to say. He practically had to press his ears closer when any team member was on the phone with Ishan just to see if he was okay.

Shubman keeps thinking about what Ishan might be doing, is he off to somewhere having fun or worse if he's lying in a bed all gloomy like him?

More often than not he finds himself thinking everything will be over once he goes back to India.

The absolute worst part is he can't seem to focus on the game he has been performing badly in practice and he's terrified about the actual game.

..........☆☆☆........

He fucked up.

Shubman knew when he saw Rohit running towards him for a run and he stayed standing there which resulted in Rohit getting out at 0.

The silence of the crowd is agitating. Shubman tries to keep himself calm as he walks back to the dressing room with his head hanging low. After getting out on just 23 runs.

He knows what is coming for him, after years he's used to it. the trolling not only from the internet but from his team members also.

Shubman sees Virat standing right next to him. He tries to give a weak smile even if it feels like dying.

Virat, standing to his right, eyes him for a second and places a hand on his shoulder while turning to look the other way. It should be reassuring, especially for Shubman who is always looking for physical affection. But today it is a reminder of a silent 'I know, I know kid'  and it makes him want to curl on the floor and cry.

He's not as strong as Virat, he's not sure if he's gonna make it out of this stadium without crumbling into pieces.

.

.

.

.

.

Shubman sits on the edge of the bed his mind replaying all the bad things that had happened in just a few days and to deal with it all he only needed one person to be with him.

It's only in those moments when he understands the drug addicts and the alcohol addicts, he understands why they need to bury themselves in alcohol or drugs. He understands it all because he himself feels addicted to someone.

Shubman knew his life would be a mess from the get-go. But he realised now how he didn't know when he got lost going so deep into the thorns However today he also realised how much he wanted to find his way back and wanted to feel happy again. Shubman didn't know how, or where to begin all he knew was that he felt a sudden feeling of urgency in his chest and a desire to run away from the thorns back to his home. He wanted to go home so bad.

In what seemed like hours he managed to type out a text and with trembling fingers, he hit the send button.

Shubman curled in the bed there he stayed staring at the screen waiting for a reply unaware of the passing time and his rapid heartbeat.

Ishan's gaze remained fixated on the glass of rum that Mayank had carefully poured for him as if it held all the answers to his problems. Lost in his memories, he barely registered the encouraging words that Mayank was offering him, 'You'll get better,' 'It's gonna be okay,' 'You'll forget him.' These words seemed hollow and meaningless, like false promises that friends feel obligated to make in times of crisis. Despite the reassurances, Ishan couldn't shake off the feeling of despair and heartbreak that had taken hold of him.

It's been weeks and Ishan is trying his best. When Mayank reached out to him to hang out, he decided to go with the hope of enjoying himself. They talked about various things, and he did manage to have some fun. However, as the night progressed and he had a glass of wine, he found himself sinking back into his gloomy state of mind. That's when Ishan pitched the idea of going back to his apartment and having some drinks and Mayank went with it.

Mayank patted Ishan's head compassionately everything was calm until Ishan finished his 4th glass that's when the tantrums came.

"Let me get some water for you" Mayank sighs as he takes Ishan's head off his shoulder pushing him back a bit.

Ishan's head falls graciously on the back pillow. He was mumbling something quietly under his breath while keeping his eyes closed and a serene look on his face.

With one last look, Mayank walked to the kitchen only to come back to the sight of Ishan sitting on the floor with his back pressed against the sofa and a black flip-flop in his arms.

"What in the" Mayank runs towards Ishan "Why in the world you're hugging a slipper"

Ishan looks up to see Mayank bending down next to him. He eyes mayank for a few seconds before bursting into crying "I bought them for Shubman" Ishan manages to say between his loud cries.

"And why do you have them here" Mayank can't help but ask.

"I found them here," Ishan says, his voice tinged with a hint of sadness, as he points his finger under the sofa, "He used to steal my slippers and try to squeeze his feet into them. So I bought him matching ones" he says with a bittersweet chuckle, reminiscing the memories.

"Then let's get rid of them if they remind you of him and make you sad" Mayank says taking the flip-flop out of Ishan's hand and throwing it across the room.

"Hey! That's Balenciaga!" Ishan made a noise of protest.

"Come on Let's restore your dehydration level there's only alcohol in your body and it's getting to your head now" Mayank says as he pours a glass of water.

"Here drink up baby" Before he could hand the glass over. Ishan is on his shoulder and the loud cries are back.

"What now I can't even call you baby? Even that reminds you of him?" Mayank tries to complain but it's no use Ishan just keeps mumbling god knows what.

"Okay okay calm down" Mayank pets Ishan's back trying to calm him down "You don't want to drink water that's okay perhaps you wanna eat something?"

"I want maggi" In a swift motion Ishan is off his shoulder with no tears in his eyes anymore.

"Um...Alright, I'll go make some" Mayank says every word carefully not wanting to have a crying Ishan on his shoulder once again.

After 15 minutes of searching the whole pantry, Mayank is standing in front of Ishan having no idea how to break the news to him that they're out of maggi or any other noodles in particular.

In what seemed like hours he managed to type out a text and with trembling fingers, he hit the send button.

Shubman curled in the bed there he stayed staring at the screen waiting for a reply unaware of the passing time and his rapid heartbeat.

"Ahh for fuck sake if you keep this up I'm gonna cry too...I'm crying" Mayank walked next to him "Move make room for me let's cry together" with both hands covering his face mayank dropped next to ishan sobbing.

After hours of struggle, Mayank finally had his peace when Ishan fell asleep surprisingly he managed to get Ishan to eat that banana after throwing a tantrum himself.

It's 4 in the morning and Mayank still can't fall asleep he's watching some TV show on the lowest volume possible feeling bored now, that is until he hears a ding on Ishan's phone that is placed on the coffee table right next to his feet.

He picks up the phone out of curiosity to see what it is. He's not even surprised after seeing it's a text from Shubman.

Mayank dialed Shubman's number quietly getting off the bed, walking towards the balcony not to disturb Ishan's rest.

The call got answered on the third ring "Hello" Mayank said calmly.

"Of course, he's with you isn't he?"Shubman asks on the the other line while Mayank just hummed in response.

"How is he-"

"Shubman do you love him?...like romantically not to be confused with platonic love." Mayank asks.

"I-- it's none of your business"

"Still can you just answer this... or not if you want to continue your life just like how it is right now" Mayank says.

"I can't let him go"

"I won't let him go" Shubman says again firmly.

"Huh, so you do love him" Mayank scoffs.

"Call me when you're back in Mumbai...and to answer your question he's a living mess he won't be replying to your text anytime soon" With that he cut the call.
