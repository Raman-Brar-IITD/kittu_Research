Ishan was sitting on the balcony of Hardik's sea-facing apartment, sipping on the lemon juice given to him after lunch. He had been resilient to eat at first — stubborn and fidgety — but the sweet aroma drifting from the kitchen and the pleading faces around him had made him change his mind.

His mind had been racing for days now, conjuring up endless possibilities, weaving webs of confusion he couldn't untangle. Now, it needed to stop. Now, he needed a clear answer. Ishan had always believed he knew himself the best. Better than anyone else. But maybe he was wrong. Perhaps he never really knew what his heart desired. Or maybe... maybe he had known it all along and had chosen to feign ignorance.

Whatever it was, it was now playing with his mind. And he didn't appreciate it at all. He had always loved Shubman and his antics. He loved how they clicked instantly, how their conversations flowed without force, how the silences between them were never heavy. He loved how Shubman understood him even without words.

He loved the way Shubman's eyes sparkled when Ishan praised his batting stance or his passion for the things he loved. But...Was he in love with Shubman? There's a difference, Ishan thought bitterly. Loving someone and being in love with someone were two entirely different things. You could love someone deeply, cry for them, care for them endlessly — and still not be in love.

So, was he in love with Shubman? This wasn't the first time he had been thinking about it.

He had been randomly tortured by these thoughts ever since that reckless confession Shubman had pulled back in England. But no matter how much he thought about it, no matter how much he analyzed and dissected it — he just couldn't seem to find his answers.

"Kya soch raha hai?" Hardik's voice pulled him out of his spiral. The older man came up behind him, voice easy but eyes sharp. Ishan startled a little, then forced a smile. "Kuch nahi bhai." "Kuch nahi?" Hardik repeated, a sly smile tugging at his lips. "Bol de na kya hua hai. Apne bhai se bhi chupayega kya?"

Ishan just gave him a look. A look that said you know what's wrong, why are you making me say it? "Pata hai," Hardik admitted easily, "par tere muh se sunna chahta hoon main." Ishan exhaled heavily, defeated. "Shubman ke baare mein soch raha hoon aur kya." Hardik's grin widened. "Aur kya soch raha hai uske baare mein? Woh bol."

"Yaar bhai..." Ishan buried his face in his hands for a second, laughing miserably. Hardik just chuckled at his sorry state. "Bol jaldi." Ishan was trapped. He couldn't lie. He couldn't run. He hesitated, then mumbled, "Main soch raha tha..." He trailed off again, struggling.  Hardik raised an eyebrow, waiting patiently.

Ishan swallowed, cheeks heating. "Do I like him? I mean, obviously, I like him — but do I like him like... he likes me? You know... am I..." He couldn't finish. He just waved his hand helplessly in the air. Hardik chuckled again — soft, not mocking.

He leaned forward. "Alright," he said, voice steady. "Let's break it down." "How would you feel if you didn't speak to him for three days straight?" Ishan frowned. "That's... we speak almost every day. Of course, I'd feel bad if we didn't." Hardik nodded, unfazed.

"Okay. How would you feel if he didn't listen to something you said?" Ishan immediately shook his head. "Bhaiya, he always listens." "Exactly." A smirk tugged at Hardik's mouth. "And how about when he looks and pays attention to someone else when you're in the same room?" Ishan looked thrown "What?"

"Okay, okay. What about when he goes somewhere without informing you first?" "Bhai..." Ishan groaned, covering his face. Hardik grinned wider. "Or when he's upset and goes to talk to someone else instead of you. How would that feel?" There was no answer. Just a slight tightening around Ishan's mouth, a frown he couldn't suppress.

Hardik watched him carefully. Then he dropped the real bomb. "What if he went on a date with someone else after waking up? How would you feel then?" Ishan froze. Hardik leaned in, voice low, searching. "Ishan... what if he wakes up one day and denies ever having feelings for you? What if he says it was just some momentary lapse because of his memory loss? How would that make you feel?"

Ishan stared into the distance. He didn't say anything. The silence stretched long and thick between them. Hardik didn't rush him. He knew silence sometimes spoke louder than words. Finally, Ishan looked up — and there was something raw in his eyes. A vulnerability he rarely showed.

Hardik sat forward. "Ishan," he said gently. "Listen to me carefully now. You're sitting here worrying about how bad you'd feel if he didn't talk to you. You're defending how he always listens to you. How he always informs you. How he always looks for you." He smiled sadly.

"Tell me something — why do you think you care this much?" Ishan opened his mouth, then shut it. No answer. Hardik didn't need one. "Bhai, this isn't friendship anymore," Hardik said softly. "This isn't just 'oh, he's my bro.' This is you feeling something you're too scared to admit to yourself."

Ishan still looked confused, still defensive. Hardik leaned back, watching him calmly. "And don't even pretend you don't know it," Hardik said. "The way your face lights up when he texts. The way you search for him in a crowded room. The way you froze when I said he might go on a date."

Ishan swallowed thickly. Hardik's voice dropped even lower. "He already confessed bhai. He already told you what you mean to him. You're the one standing here pretending it's still just friendship." Ishan finally met his gaze — wide-eyed, lost.

"Friends don't look at friends the way you look at him," Hardik whispered. "Friends don't feel sick imagining someone else touching them. And friends damn sure don't hurt this much over the thought of losing them." Silence. The air felt electric.

"Accept it," Hardik said. "You're already gone for him. You just have to catch up with your own heart." Ishan's throat worked, struggling to swallow. Hardik leaned forward again. "Answer me this: Why does it hurt you so much when you imagine him with someone else?" Ishan shifted, uncomfortable.

"I don't know... it would just feel weird." "Really?" Hardik smirked. "Would you feel 'weird' if it were me?" Ishan blinked fast. "No... but you're different—" "Exactly." Hardik's grin turned triumphant. "Because it's not friendship with him. It's more." Ishan opened his mouth to argue — but no words came. He hated how much sense Hardik made.

"You're really gonna sit here and pretend it's normal, huh?" Hardik said, exasperated. "Normal friends don't get jealous. Normal friends don't stare at each other like they hung the damn stars." Ishan looked away stubbornly. "I don't know what I feel..." His voice cracked a little. "It's confusing. He forgot me, bhaiya. And even then... he found his way back to me."

Hardik's expression softened. "Baccha," he murmured. "Don't you get it? Even when his memories were wiped — even when fate tried to reset everything — his heart still chose you." He tapped Ishan's chest lightly. "Because it was always you." Ishan blinked rapidly, tears threatening.

"But what if he regrets it later?" he whispered. Hardik smiled sadly. "If forgetting you didn't change his heart... remembering sure as hell won't either." Ishan bit his lip hard, looking completely, utterly lost. Hardik sighed dramatically.

"Yaar, how dumb are you?" he said with mock scorn. "The man fell for you twice — from scratch. Meanwhile, you're sitting here acting like you caught feelings because of bad biryani." Ishan laughed — a broken, watery sound — cheeks turning pink. "You're scared because it's real," Hardik said quietly. "Because it's big. Because once you admit it — there's no pretending anymore."

Ishan stared at him, eyes wide and raw. Hardik's voice softened even more. "Stop thinking.

Stop being scared. You already know what you feel. You've always known. You were just waiting for him to say it first." "And now that he has," Hardik said, standing up and ruffling Ishan's hair affectionately, "you're out here acting like a goat who wandered into a science exam."

Ishan laughed helplessly, tears slipping out despite himself. Hardik smiled. "Go to him, idiot," he said fondly. "Go show him you love him too. You already have — in every way that matters." Ishan stood up too, heart pounding, adrenaline rushing through his veins. Hope blooming wild and fierce inside his chest.

Maybe he didn't have the perfect words. Maybe he didn't have all the answers yet. But he had one truth, blazing clear and undeniable. It was always him. It had always been Shubman. And now, finally, he was ready to chase what was his.
