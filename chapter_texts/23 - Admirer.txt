He was staring intently at Ishan, who stood in the corner of the hospital room, engaged in a flurry of words over the phone. No. Ishan Kishan was breathtakingly beautiful, crush or not. That wasn't up for debate. That was the very first thing Shubman had realized after losing his memory.

He remembered seeing Ishan's face that day and thinking, almost absentmindedly, just how pretty he was. He had brushed it off as a simple, harmless observation. Nothing more. Nothing deeper. But it had turned out that Shubman had always been hopelessly in love with him.

Now, as he sat and watched Ishan pace with frustration painted on his face, Shubman knew that Ishan wasn't just beautiful—he was beautiful that had poems written about them. And if only Shubman were talented enough, he would have written a few himself.

He had certainly tried. He thought about it more times than he could count. But every time he sat down to write something, the words would betray him. They'd feel clumsy, insufficient, and painfully unworthy. He wasn't sure if it was his lack of poetic skill or if it was that the words themselves didn't dare to insult Ishan's beauty by trying to describe it.

Maybe that was it. Maybe Ishan didn't need words to be described. Perhaps the very concept of beauty had needed someone like Ishan to finally make sense. Perhaps the words weren't shy—they were simply speechless in front of him.

And now, with his memories restored, Shubman was grateful. Because he never wanted to forget anything that had Ishan in it. Not a single thing. He wanted to remember every little detail. He wanted to know every quirk, every favorite food, every dream, and every fear. He wanted to tattoo Ishan's preferences onto his mind.

He wanted to wake up every morning and look at Ishan's face as he slept beside him. He wanted to memorize the way his eyes looked when they were closed. Those lashes, those lids. He wanted to learn the exact curve of them—the slight almond shape of his eyes when they were tucked away behind tan, soft lids.

He wanted to memorize the gentle arc of Ishan's lashes as they cast shadows across his cheeks, to study the quiet stillness in his expression when he was asleep. Even then, even with his fire tucked away, Ishan still looked powerful. Like a resting god.

That image—of Ishan's closed eyes, peaceful and perfect—was one Shubman wanted to be braided into the strands of his memory. He wanted to be able to paint thousands of portraits, each one capturing the silent grace of those eyelids. The unspoken serenity of a man who was so alive, so vibrant, even when still.

Ishan had that kind of silent beauty. The kind you didn't gawk at—you just observed in awe. Shubman had always been one of those observers. But unlike the rest of the world, he wasn't just another passerby in Ishan's orbit. No, he took pride in being the first one—the first observer. He was a worshipper.

He wanted to outline the shape of Ishan's perfect nose with his fingers, to press his palm gently to those warm cheeks every morning. But he couldn't. As close as they were, Shubman didn't have that right. He wasn't allowed to touch Ishan like that. Not yet. So, instead, he stared.

Stared until he memorized every bump and ridge of Ishan's face. Stared until he could trace the location of every mole, every tiny freckle with his eyes closed. He had always been silently observing Ishan. Always appreciating him in his own quiet way. Shubman had always been the first to wake up between the two of them.

Not because he was a morning person—he was anything but that—but because he didn't want to waste a single chance to watch Ishan sleep. Silently appreciate that serene beauty. He wasn't an addict, but maybe he was addicted to Ishan. To his beauty, his warmth, his everything. Maybe he didn't fully understand the fine line between admiration and obsession when it came to him—but could you blame him? It was Ishan Kishan.

"Pretty, isn't he?" The voice was soft and delicate, but it startled Shubman out of his thoughts. He turned to see a woman standing a little distance away. Her expression was serene, almost fond, and her eyes were fixed on Ishan with an emotion that mirrored his own.

She had dimples on her cheeks and soft features that made her look kind. Shubman braced himself for the sharp pang of jealousy—but it didn't come. Instead, he found himself smiling. "The prettiest," he said quietly, sincerely. She glanced at him, and their eyes met. Dimples met dimples in a silent agreement.

"Hi. I'm Ira," she said, offering her hand. "I was the assistant doctor on your case." "Hello, Ira. I'm Shubman." He shook her hand gratefully. Her grip was warm, grounding. There was a kind of mutual understanding between them—a shared reverence for the boy standing in the corner of the room, still arguing passionately over the phone.

Ira had the kind presence that the family craved while their loved ones fought life-threatening situations inside the OT. She had a calm presence and an indelible personality. Her smile held a silent promise, and her hands were ready to hold onto any shivering ones. She looked like the person you could gossip with all you wanted, but she also looked like the person who would fight for you until the end. Her presence held a sense of calm in the chaos.

Shubman would usually be jealous of anyone who imitated that soft fondness for Ishan on anyone else. With Ira, on the other hand, he didn't feel threatened at all. He didn't feel the need to protect Ishan or the need to prove anything at all. When she looked at him, he felt seen, as if she knew his deepest secrets. And although he felt so exposed, he didn't feel threatened. In fact, he felt understood.

He felt as if he didn't need to explain himself for her to understand. He felt as if she knew. She couldn't possibly have known anything, but he felt it anyway. He didn't hate this feeling at all. Ira smiled at him in appreciation. He didn't know if he was being appreciated for recovering or if it was because of Ishan.

Shubman's eyes immediately landed on Ishan, who was now looking right at him. Their eyes met, and Shubman passed him a small smile before turning back to her. "What?" he feigned confusion. He knew he couldn't lie to her, but he tried anyway.

"Oh, please, Shubman. Everyone knows," she chuckled, like an adult amused by a child trying to lie about breaking a vase. "Knows what?" He gave her his best-confused expression. She just smiled, dimples deepening. "Knows how head over heels you are for him."

Shubman choked on plain air. God, he hadn't expected her to be that blunt. "You aren't one to beat around the bush, I see," he said, swallowing his embarrassment. "Aww, there's nothing to be embarrassed about. I mean, I completely get you. It's Ishan, after all." She looked over at Ishan with the same fondness again, and Shubman turned to her, curious.

Is there anything else even worthy of admiration?" And Shubman couldn't help but agree. "Since when have you been this 'admirer'?" he asked, brow furrowed.

"Since his Under-19 days. Wish I knew him even before that," she said, almost dazed. "I remember the day they brought you in after your accident. I was the one who received your barely breathing body. You were my first severe case, Shubman, and I was just as scared as the friends who had brought you."

"I didn't understand why everyone was panicking or why I was ordered to shift you to the VIP section. I just knew I had a patient to save. But then, I saw him. Sitting outside your room, looking like the life had been sucked out of him. Like he'd just lived through the worst day of his life."

"And then I saw you—really saw you. Shubman Gill. And suddenly, everything made sense. But that wasn't why you became my priority. You became my priority because of the way he looked at you as if his breath was tied to yours."

"You don't sound like just an admirer. Have you met him before?" Shubman asked. "I have," she smiled. "When?" he asked quickly. Ira chuckled.

"It was just for a moment. I was never into cricket. My family would watch matches while I'd escape into chores. I thought it was the most overrated game in existence," she said, smirking at his offended expression. "So how did you know him from his Under-19 days?"

"Cricket suddenly mattered. Every six he hit stitched my heart a little more. I didn't even know his full name back then. Just that he was Ishan, a cricketer. That was enough. And when I saw him that day, lifeless outside your room, it hit me hard. The light in him had dimmed."

"So I gave everything I had to save you, Shubman. Because you matter to him. And anything that matters to Ishan Kishan automatically matters to me."

Shubman had unshed tears in his eyes. He had always known that Ishan was sunshine personified, and he had also known that Ishan had saved him at bad times before. But there was a difference between trying to lighten up your best friend's life and trying to heal a complete stranger. Shubman was so in love with Ishan that it wasn't even possible to fall more for him, but in this moment he fell more anyway.

He looked at Ishan with the most delicate look, as if he would disappear in thin air if he stared a bit too hard.  Ira looked back at Ishan and smiled to herself. "You're lucky." "I am," Shubman agreed, his eyes finding Ishan again. "I really am." He didn't even try to question it at all. He was the luckiest, and he took pride in accepting it.

They both stood in silence for a moment, watching Ishan. He was no longer talking—just standing there, running a hand through his hair, visibly exhausted. He looked over and caught Shubman's gaze. For a second, their eyes locked, and everything else faded away.

Ishan walked over to him, the tiredness in his expression immediately melting into something softer as he approached. "Everything's done. We can leave in fifteen," he said. "Good," Shubman said, trying to keep his tone casual despite the flutter in his chest. Ishan gave him a curious look, then added, "You okay?" Shubman smiled up at him. "Never better."

Ishan didn't look convinced at all, but he decided to let it go for the moment. Then his gaze landed on Ira, who was standing behind Shubman with glossy eyes. She immediately passed him a smile as their eyes met. "You have dimples." Ishan pointed at the fact, and her dimples deepened.

"Oh. Sorry, it's just that you were the doctor who helped throughout this process, and I know you, but I hadn't realized that you had dimples until this moment, and I just point at it. I just—" Ishan started to frantically explain when he realized how awkward it sounded. Ira's laugh cut Ishan's uncalled rant short.

She extended her hands towards his head but then frowned a bit and pulled her hand back. "I am Ira, by the way." She extended the same towards Ishan for a hand, and he shook it with a smile. "That name sounds familiar. Have we met before?" And nobody had to know if her heart did a somersault at that. Shubman gave her a knowing look, but Ishan didn't dwell on that any longer.

"It's just you look familiar. Anyway, you have helped a lot. Thank you." Ishan shook her hand a bit more, and she smiled gratefully at him. "Happy to help." He took Shubman's file. She took a quick look and left the room, throwing Shubman a look from over her shoulder. Shubman didn't realize what that look meant until after he reached his home and opened his file. She had left him a note inside.
