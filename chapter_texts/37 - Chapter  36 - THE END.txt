•SHUBMAN GILL•

India needs 10 runs from two balls, which means the batsman either has to hit two sixes or one six and one four. The tension is palpable in the air. We have to win this; after all, it's the ODI World Cup final.

Shreyas is on strike. It's literally a do-or-die situation for him. He looks quite composed from the outside, but I can only imagine the kind of pressure he must be feeling right now. The weight of a billion hopes presses down on his shoulders.

The crowd falls into an anxious silence as Pat Cummins strides in to bowl the 5th ball of the last over. Shreyas's eyes lock onto the ball, and with a powerful swing, he sends it soaring over the boundary for a six. The stadium erupts. Just four runs now separate us from the victory.

The final ball is delivered. It's not quite short enough for a six but could make a four. Maxwell lunges toward the ball in an attempt to stop it from hitting the boundary. Everything seems to happen in slow motion. The nerves are too much to handle, and then it happens..........Maxwell's hand only grazes the ball but can't stop it, and the ball rolls and hits the boundary.

The entire stadium erupts in loud cheers, hoots, and claps. People start chanting slogans of various kinds, and fireworks burst in the night sky. Shreyas and Jaddu bhai who was the non-striker end, run toward each other for hugs. From the dugout, we all leap up, charging onto the field. The moment is surreal and can't be described in words. The adrenaline is spiking.

All the teammates hug each other, congratulate one another, and dance silly in the middle of the ground. Around us, an ocean of blue waves tricolour flags. There's something magical about this victory, securing an ICC trophy after so many years. It's about making those who supported us through thick and thin proud. The feeling is priceless, beyond the reach of words.

As the team parades with the trophy, thanking the crowd for their unwavering support, I feel a hard ring case in my pocket.

Today, I’m going to propose.

I coax Ishan towards the pitch, signaling to Nia. She distracts him with some light conversation, giving me the chance to kneel and pull out the ring box. Nia gently turns Ishan around and nudges him toward me. Confusion flashes on his face until he sees me kneeling. His eyes widen comically, almost ready to pop out of their sockets.

It's surprising that his eyeballs don't roll out from their sockets.

He freezes, his expression stunned. Usually, I'm the one who misses the obvious, but this time, without a doubt, I can say that Ishan is the oblivious one. Over the past month, I’ve dropped hundreds of hints about my intentions to propose. One night, while he was asleep, I even slipped the ring onto his finger to check the fit.

I smile at him, my heart pounding in my chest. "When I am with you," I begin, "the world becomes a more beautiful place. I start seeing beauty in everything. I never thought that someone's hugs could be this addictive until I hugged you. I never believed that love could be this beautiful until I fell in love with you. I like this version of myself that you’ve brought out."

Tears well up in his eyes as he listens, and I see the emotion mirrored in his expression. The world around us fades away momentarily as if it’s just the two of us in this moment. The fireworks are still going on, adding more beauty to the moment.

I hear the crowd loudly cheering. Fireworks continue to burst in the background, colouring the night sky. The chilly but tolerable November breeze brushes against us, adding a touch of coolness to the atmosphere. The moment feels surreal, as if we’re living in a dream.

Nia, Dev, Kuldeep, and Yuzi Bhai stand a little distance from us, each holding a poster with a single word on it.

Instead of reading:

WILL        YOU         MARRY          ME?

it reads :

YOU         WILL       MARRY          ME!

And this isn't even a mistake; it's very much intentional. One can clearly see the exclamation mark after the word "ME" when I was discussing the proposal with Nia. She came up with this idea because she has the habit of coming up with the most dramatic ones. She said, "Ishan doesn't really have an option, so it shouldn't be a question rather, a declaration that he has to marry you."

But then again, the fake dating idea was hers, and I just couldn't thank her enough for that. At that time, the idea looked so bizarre, but see how everything turned out. If someone had told me at that time that I would be proposing to Ishan by the end of the year, I would have laughed at it.

Ishan gently takes my hand and gestures for me to stand up. As I rise, he tiptoes and wraps his arms around my shoulders, pulling me into a tight embrace. His warmth envelops me, and I reciprocate, my arms encircling him as I tenderly rub his back.

"Is this happening for real?" he whispers softly, breaking the hug just enough to look into my eyes. I simply nod, my smile growing wider.

"So," I say, opening the box and taking out the ring, "will you be my Shatak? Forever?" A warmth spreads through me. The crowd’s cheers fade into the background as I wait for his response.

"Yes." His eyes glint with pure happiness as he extends his hand. Taking the cue, I slip the ring onto his ring finger. It fits perfectly. Ishan studies the ring. It's actually a platinum band with two bats engraved on it, one slightly taller than the other.

More amusement flickers in his eyes when he realizes that the bats engraved in the rings indicate us, the taller one being me and the shorter one him. "You like it?" I whisper.

"I love it," he says, gently holding my neck and drawing me closer. His warm, soft lips meet mine, and I part my lips, melting into the kiss. I grasp his waist firmly, pulling him even closer, as if we aren't already pressed against each other.

The kiss feels enchanting, the emotions I hold for him surging even more at this moment.

Breaking the kiss slowly, I look into his eyes and say, "I know it's a little cliché, but let me say it." He cradles my face gently in his hands, and I continue, my arms still wrapped around his waist. "You are my home, the one I want to return to every day, every moment, always."

"You are the one I want to be clingy with, the one I want to annoy with my antics. You are the only one I want to spend the rest of my life with. No matter what changes the future may bring, I promise to stand by your side through every situation."

He gazes deeply into my eyes and replies, "You are the only one I allow to be clingy with me. Your antics are the only ones I find truly endearing. You are the only one I want by my side, now and forever."

They say everything happens for a reason, and looking back, I can't help but believe it's true. My life took an unexpected turn when I lied to my family about having a boyfriend. It was a small lie, meant to ease their constant worries about my love life. Little did I know, that lie would set off a chain of events that would change my life forever.

I spotted him sitting alone at a corner table, waiting for someone. Before I could second-guess myself, I approached him and asked if he would pretend to be my boyfriend. To my surprise, he agreed.

After a few days, an article appeared, claiming we were dating. It spread like wildfire, and soon everyone believed the story. Nia suggested we continue the rumors to keep up appearances. It seemed harmless enough.

In the course of fake dating him, I didn't realize when I started having very real feelings for him. And those feelings made their presence felt when Ishan kissed me that day in the wedding hall, when one of the bridesmaids challenged him. In that moment, everything became clear.

And then it turns out he harbors the same feelings for me. I will never forget the night when he confessed to me, kissed me, and made me feel loved.

Today, as I stand before him, the man who was once just a teammate in a restaurant, I can hardly believe how far we've come. Every event led us to this moment where I proposed to him.

He is no longer just my boyfriend but my fiancé, my soon-to-be husband.

As I look back on all the twists and turns, the lies, the laughter, the tears and the love, I realize how grateful I am for every single moment. Everything truly does happen for a reason, and my reason is standing right here, my love, my shatak, my Ishan.

I lean in and peck his lips. "I love you."

He pecks my lips back. "Always."
