It was a warm evening at the Gill residence. The sun had dipped behind the horizon, leaving behind a golden trail, and fairy lights lit up the garden like stars had rained down just for tonight. Everyone was there—both families, friends, and teammates—brought together for what was supposed to be a simple dinner. Shubman's mother, of course, had turned it into a full-fledged celebration.

Last week, after the confession, Shubman proposed letting the family know. He had always wanted to include the family in on this but had been too afraid; he didn't have to, he realized after that one conversation. That conversation with Ishan had all of Shubman's insecurities fade, and he loved it.

When he told his family that he wanted to host a small lawn party here in Chandigarh for all of his friends before another tournament came up and they couldn't make time anymore, his sister had looked at him suspiciously, almost reading his mind. And his mother, there was a weird glint in her eyes, as if she thought that it was the most brilliant idea one could come up with.

"I'll call Pallavi then." She said. And when a very flustered Shubman asked her why she was calling her, she added, "Why? Ishan's family would join us, right?" as if it was the most obvious thing in the world. His father had just looked up from the newspaper in his hand, meeting eyes with not Shubman but Ishan, right across the room where Ishan was sitting on the sofa.

Just like that, everything was set. They decided to invite their friends to Chandigarh as a thank-you—for tolerating Shubman when even he couldn't tolerate himself. The plan was for everyone to fly down the following week, primarily because, in Shubman's mother's words, "I need at least a week to prepare, Shubman. I'm not a heathen who'll let you throw a party with plastic chairs and stale chips. Unlike you, some of us still have a taste—and a shred of civic sense left." Nobody dared argue after that. Not even Ishan, who had nearly choked laughing.

And here they were—a week later—gathered in the backyard, the air buzzing with conversation and the aroma of food. Everyone had already helped themselves to generous servings, plates filled and spirits high, as laughter echoed from every corner of the lawn. The atmosphere was warm, lively, and exactly what they all needed—uncomplicated joy.

Ishan stood near the far end of the backyard, in his black shirt, looking almost suspiciously calm as he helped Viraj tie tiny flags across a low railing. His movements were composed, but his eyes kept flicking to the house, searching for one particular face. While he knew that there was nothing that could possibly go wrong, there was still a flicker of doubt that he couldn't shut up.

This was, without a doubt, one of the most serious conversations of his life—and Ishan Kishan didn't do serious. At least, not when it came to himself. He was the loudest laugh in the room, the king of comebacks and chaotic energy. He could sit through anyone else's storms, offer solid advice with unexpected maturity, and still crack a joke to ease the tension.

But the moment the seriousness pointed toward him, he shut down. Ishan could carry the world's weight for others, but when it came to his own, he pretended it wasn't there. That's why, at this moment, he was zoning out—until he saw him. The love of his life.

Shubman walked out finally, wearing a soft pastel shirt and that slightly nervous smile he wore only when something big was coming. Ishan caught the look, the silent understanding, and straightened. "Ready?" he mouthed. Shubman nodded. Barely. They walked towards each other, and the world seemed to hold no importance right at that moment.

As their hands met in a silent promise, the noise around them faded into a distant hum. Ishan's fingers were warm—slightly trembling despite the steady facade he wore like armor—and Shubman's grip tightened, grounding them both. Shubman smiled, soft and breathless like the universe had just granted him his most reckless wish. Ishan, who had always been louder than the silence in any room, said nothing—but his eyes, god, his eyes spoke volumes.

Shubman leaned in, forehead brushing against Ishan's for the briefest second. "We've got this, right?" Ishan huffed a quiet laugh, all nerves and wonder. "We always did." And that was all they needed. Not grand declarations, not rehearsed speeches. Just this quiet, burning certainty that no matter who stood in front of them—family, friends, the world—they'd face it together. Hand in hand. Always.

As they walked towards the group sitting comfortably at the big table in the middle of the lawn, the chatter around them fell to a hush. Shubman cleared his throat. "I just—um—thank you, all of you, for being here." A few heads tilted. No one was expecting a speech, but they all felt the tension building up. "I know tonight was just supposed to be dinner, but there's something I want to say. Or... we want to say."

Their hands were intertwined by their side in a loud, unapologetic declaration—a silent yet unmistakable statement that needed no words because everyone could see exactly what they were trying to say. No one pulled away, and no one spoke. After a minute or so, Shubman continued.

"I lost my memories a while ago," he said, looking at Ishan first and then at the crowd. "And in that process, I also remembered what it feels like to fall in love... again." There were gasps around the table, but Shubman wasn't listening. Not when the only one he ever wanted was so within reach. And so, he continued, "With the same person. And to realize that no matter how many times the world resets, I'd still find my way back to him."

Ishan's fingers curled around Shubman's tighter, grounding him. He took one look at Shubman and knew that he was getting closer to his limit, so much for 'I'll tell them.' He shook his head with a small smile and pulled Shubman back towards himself. Taking over the half-completed speech.

"We have been fighting the battles for some time now, which were honestly just—stupid. And now, we are not anymore." He smiled at the hopeful gaze with which everyone looked at him. He knew one thing at that moment: this wasn't them coming out or declaring their love; this was just them accepting what was already too obvious to everyone else, and the thought of it made him laugh.

So, he did. He chuckled, and that made the most brilliant sound there was. "I know that most of the people sitting around here would go like, 'I knew it' or 'I told you,' and yes, you would be a hundred percent right, but we would still like to say it." And he looked towards Shubman with an encouraging gaze. "Ishan and I... we're together. And have been—quietly—for a while. But today, we don't want to hide anymore."

The first response came not from a friend, but from Shahneel, who dramatically shouted, "Finally!" and then clapped hard enough to startle a few birds. She looked like she had just earned the most important promotion of her life. Her eyes were wet, and her lips pulled into a wide smile. Cheers broke out after that. Ishan's mother had tears in her eyes as she got up and pulled both boys into a hug. Shubman's father simply smiled and said, "About time."

The rest of the evening unfolded like something out of a dream. The two families sat together, laughing over old stories, trading embarrassing childhood secrets, and fussing over both boys like they hadn't just made one of the biggest announcements of their lives.

Rishabh raised a toast halfway through. "To the idiot who forgot the love of his life and still fell for him again. And to the guy who was so oblivious that it would put even the stupidest of rocks to shame." Everyone laughed. Hardik looked over at Ishan with a glint in his eyes and added, "I mean, I would've paid money to see Shubman confess like that again. Shame we didn't record it."

Shubman rolled his eyes at that while Ishan just rested his head on Shubman's shoulder, relaxed in a way he hadn't been in months. Abhishek, who was quiet for a while, said with the sweetest smile on his face. "Took you long enough. I was starting to lose money on my bets." "You bet on our relationship?" Shubman asked, horrified.

"Please. I started a poll," Rishabh said proudly. "And guess who won? Me, obviously. I always knew this would happen." Siraj, who had left a minute ago, walked in right at that moment with his phone in his hand. "Say cheese, lovebirds!" he shouted. "Delete that or I'll hit you with my bat," Ishan warned, though his smile gave him away.

"He is not deleting anything; this picture will go down in history along with all the other pictures that could be the personification of 'heart eyes,' 'longing but not trying,' 'Am I in love with my best friend,' And 'fuck the world, let me stare at my world' moments of Shubman captured on all of our camera rolls," Abhishek said with raised eyebrows like it was some sort of challenge.

"You did not just say that." Rishabh joined Hardik in the most ridiculous laughter dare. "Are you accepting that you have been stalking us like a creep?" Shubman said, gaze rooted on Abhishek in a heated glare. "Stalking is when people dig up something about someone that is not public information Shubman, not when you sit right in front of people and media giving Ishan heart eyes."

"Stalking is definitely not possible in a scenario where we don't have to dig anything up but the subject is stupid enough to sit in a room filled with cameras and stare right at his best friend when he is not even the one speaking, so no, Shubman, this is not stalking; this is, in fact, an active observing of facts that are too obvious to ignore," Mayank said it with the straightest face ever, and that should have made people laugh, but it scared a few of them.

After a minute of glaring between Shubman and Mayank, Rohit spoke. "I mean, Shubi did confess most dramatically ever. Right on the field, too. Classic 'Main Hoon Na' vibes," he said, laughing. "Can we not bring that up again?" Shubman groaned, hiding behind his hands.

"Nope, that story is part of cricket folklore now," Virat said, grinning. "Future players will train with 'How Not to Confess Your Love in Front of Your Teammates While You Have No Recollection of Your Memories' as part of the syllabus."

"But I will say this," Virat continued, looking at both of them with a gentle expression, "it's rare to find something this genuine. You two—" he paused, eyes warm, "you've got something good. Don't let the world mess it up." And everyone looked at them as if they were the most precious thing in the room; it melted Ishan's heart. Even Mayank looked at Shubman with pride in his eyes, and that was saying something.

Later, as the night slipped into a gentle hush, the backyard glowed with fairy lights and lanterns. Blankets were spread out for people to sit on. Someone had even lit a bonfire. People laughed over shared memories, Ishan's mom brought out homemade laddoos, and someone turned on a speaker with soft music playing from the corner.

Shubman sat next to Ishan, fingers lightly brushing over the back of his hand. "This... this feels unreal," he whispered. "It's very real," Ishan replied. "And it's ours." "I thought it would be harder. That they'd ask questions or say something... I don't know. Heavy."

"Well, Shubi, I told you we didn't have to let them know and make them understand. They already knew. They had known since before you or me. They were just waiting for us to realize. We just had to let them know that we finally did." Shubman smiled, looking around him. The love and acceptance he got from the people around him had made his heart ache a bit. In a good way, though.

He had never thought he deserved any of this. He thought that he would be loathed, cursed, and shamed if anyone ever knew about his sexuality. For the first few months of him realizing his feelings for Ishan, Shubman had felt that everyone he had ever known and every relationship he had ever formed would just crumble to dust if they ever knew who he really was. He should have had some faith in the people he loved so dearly, he realized.

Ishan looked at him as if he knew what was going on in his mind. His eyes were warm with love, and his lips curled with the tease at the tip of his tongue. "Well, now that you're dating me, tell me, how does it feel to finally achieve all your dreams?" Ishan winked. "God, you're annoying," Shubman muttered fondly. "I'm your annoying," Ishan shot back, eyes gleaming.

The teasing from the friends continued until long after midnight. Someone passed around old photos from when they were in the under-19 camp. "Oh my God, remember this?" Rishabh cackled. "This was the day Ishan dropped a catch and blamed the sun." "That sun was evil that day," Ishan said defensively.

"And this," Shahneel pointed to another, "this was when Shubman tried to be cool and tripped over his own shoelaces." "Hey—" "You were trying to impress Ishan," she added, and the entire group dissolved into laughter again.

By the time everyone was too full to move and too happy to care, the stars were bright above, and the night had turned cold enough for people to wrap themselves in shawls and blankets. The fire crackled. Someone had started humming a tune.

Ishan looked at Shubman, eyes reflecting the golden light. "You okay?" he asked. Shubman nodded. "For the first time in a long time, I really am." "Even with all the teasing?" "Especially with the teasing. It means they love us." Ishan leaned in, pressing a soft kiss to the corner of his mouth. "Good. Because I love us too."

And under the stars, surrounded by family, friends, warmth, and laughter, they sat—hands entwined, hearts light, and futures quietly unfolding.
