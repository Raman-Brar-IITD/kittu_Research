As soon as Ishan arrived at the hospital, the nurse guided him to the room where Shubman was being kept under observation. It took Ishan three hours to fly and another hour by cab to reach the hospital, but Shubman still hadn't awakened. "What's wrong?" Ishan asked as he joined the other members of the group.

"Nothing. Everything is fine," Hardik replied too quickly. Ishan looked at Hardik with a questioning gaze that seemed to say, 'I want the truth, not this "everything is fine" nonsense.' "Really, Ishan, you can speak to the doctors if you want," Virat interjected. "Yeah, thank you," Ishan said, and he went to find the doctor in charge of Shubman's care.

The doctor explained, "There's nothing we can say for sure at this moment, but he is not in danger. He collapsed due to a sudden rush of memories, and he will wake up when his brain is ready to process them again. It's similar to how your phone sometimes shuts down because of software issues. I specifically instructed everyone not to trigger his memories deliberately and to allow him to regain them at his own pace."

"I'm relieved that he has regained them naturally. Had those memories been forced, he might have ended up in a persistent vegetative state. Since they have come back to him at his own pace, there's no need to worry about his condition. However, I can't estimate how long it will take for him to wake up. I assure you it won't be more than a few weeks."

"A FEW WEEKS?" Ishan exclaimed, his mouth agape and his eyes wide as saucers. "Well, that is the estimated timeframe; it doesn't mean he will be unconscious for that long. He might wake up in a few days—or even a few hours—but nothing can be said for sure."

"How is that different from being in a coma?" "His brain is responding like a normal unconscious person's would, so he isn't in any immediate danger."

When Ishan approached the doctor to inquire about Shubman's condition, he hadn't considered this possibility at all. He had come prepared for the worst-case scenario, and what the doctor had shared was far from the terrible outcome he imagined. Yet, strangely enough, it didn't ease his anxiety. Ishan wished the doctor would say that Shubman might wake up in an hour or that he was already awake, just under observation.

He couldn't bring himself to believe in this vegetative state nonsense. The rational part of his mind kept reminding him that, at least, Shubman wasn't in danger and that he would be okay. But, in that moment, Ishan found it hard to think rationally. He didn't want to be rational; he simply wanted to see Shubman wake up and be alright.

"Don't listen to him; it's his hobby to scare people," a gentle voice said from behind him. When Ishan turned around, he was faced with a calm expression belonging to a female doctor.

"What?" he asked. "Don't worry about what he said. When they first brought the patient in, he told the family that Shubman's brain wasn't responding and that they might have caused this condition. Poor mother nearly fainted."

She glanced at the retreating figure of the doctor she had just spoken about, disappointment evident in her eyes. "He's not a good person, really. I feel bad for his patients. But trust me, he's an excellent doctor. Your friend will wake up in no more than three days under his care. It could even happen in a few hours. So, don't worry too much."

"He told Shubman's mother that they might have caused this state for her son," Ishan added. The doctor chuckled lightly. "Yes, he did. Believe me, I wanted to slap him too." "Where are they now?" Ishan asked, almost in a daze. He recalled the phone call from Shubman's mom, her voice shaking.

"They're in the family room. I suggested they rest for a bit. The boys arrived a few hours ago, but the family has been here much longer. I wanted them to go home for a while, but they insisted on staying. You should try to convince them to go home; I think they'd listen to you." She smiled warmly and pointed in the direction of the family room.

Ishan walked towards the room, not fully aware of his surroundings. He could hear the words being spoken, but he couldn't process them; he simply followed the suggested direction in a daze. "Are you okay?" He turned to see the same doctor holding his upper arm, looking at him with concern. "Yeah." That was all he could muster at the moment.

When he had arrived at the hospital earlier, he could still form complete sentences and think clearly, but now it felt like that ability had slipped away from him. After observing Ishan for a minute or two, the doctor called over a nurse and handed her the file she was holding. Without a word, she guided Ishan toward a door marked 'Family Room.'

As he entered, the first thing that caught his eye was Shahneel, wiping away her tears. Next, he noticed Shubman's mother asleep on a couch, while his father stood over her, texting someone. The doctor cleared her throat behind him, and as soon as she had their attention, she quietly exited the room, closing the door behind her.

Shahneel ran up and hugged him tightly, while Shubman's father gently led them both out of the room to avoid disturbing the sleeping mother. "You came," Shahneel exclaimed; it wasn't a question, but rather a given. Of course, he came. Ishan had never seen Shahneel this upset before; even when Shubman had gotten into the accident, Shahneel had remained the calmest person in the room.

"What's wrong?" Ishan asked, concerned. "Nothing. It was just all a bit too overwhelming." He sat there with them, asking about how they had found him unconscious. After a while, Shahneel's mother came out of the room, panicking when she found no one there with her upon waking up.

It took some time to console her; she looked frantic. Once she calmed down and saw Ishan standing there, she hugged him immediately. Ishan comforted her and convinced both of them to leave the hospital for now. He assured Shubman's parents that he would take care of everything and that they should go get some rest.

The doctor had been right after all; he successfully managed to send them home when everyone else had tried and failed. They listened to him without question. Shahneel had to be somewhere important but had been late because of this accident, so she left to wrap up her work before returning to the hospital.

It had been three hours since Ishan arrived at the hospital, and time felt both too fast and too slow. He couldn't comprehend a word anyone was saying, and the entire episode of Shubman's family leaving went by in a daze. He didn't remember what any of them had said; in fact, he didn't even recall what he himself had said.

He was tired and hungry, but he also felt nauseous and sleepless. "Sleep, or you'll be the next one to be admitted," came a calm voice from behind him. He turned to see the doctor again, her face unfamiliar. Ishan prided himself on remembering faces instantly, but this one eluded him. He recognized her by her voice. As he continued to stare at her, struggling to understand, she guided him toward Hardik.

"Feed him, please; he's seconds away from fainting," she urged, and all of his teammates glanced at him with concern. They did their best to make Ishan sit and coax him into eating. They might have succeeded, but he had no recollection of it—only of drifting off into a haze of sleep.

After meeting the doctor, everything became a blur. He vaguely remembered waking up on a couch and, upon opening his eyes, saw Shubman lying unconscious a few feet away on a hospital bed. Ishan had no idea how he ended up in Shubman's hospital room. A few minutes later, several doctors entered while he continued to gaze at Shubman in a daze.

Noticing the doctors approaching his bed, he observed their every move and expression. "Here, drink this," one of them said, breaking his intense staring. He turned his head to the right and saw the same doctor from earlier sitting beside him with a glass of strange-smelling liquid.

"What?" was all Ishan managed to say. "Drink this. You'll feel better. You were too exhausted yesterday, both physically and mentally. You need to recover," she explained, smiling as if talking to a child. Ishan appreciated being treated that way at that moment. He took the glass and drank it in one gulp. Surprisingly, it didn't taste as foul as it smelled.

Feeling refreshed, he wanted to ask what it was, but he was also tempted to close his eyes and just sit in peace. When he opened his eyes again, the other doctors had left the room, and the female doctor was checking on Shubman.

"Is he better?" Ishan asked. The doctor smiled. "Yes, he will regain consciousness today, if my estimation is correct. And, not to boast, but I have never been wrong with my estimations before." For the first time since the previous day, Ishan genuinely smiled. Now fully aware of his surroundings, he could process everything, and that felt good.

"You know, when he was brought in yesterday, he was unconscious but actively screaming at the top of his lungs," the doctor continued. "Hmm?" Ishan replied, intrigued. "Well, he seemed to be regaining his memories. He was saying a lot of things that didn't make any sense—just random words and phrases he might have heard or said throughout his life."

"This kind of response is not uncommon in such conditions. We doctors are always prepared for situations like this. Along with the random phrases, he was also mentioning names of people he may have known. However..." She glanced at Ishan, smiling. "But?" he asked, now fully invested in the conversation.

"There was one name he mentioned so frequently that it stuck in our minds. Don't get me wrong; it's common for someone in his state to repeat a single name, but he wasn't just saying it—he seemed to be having a conversation that we couldn't understand because it wasn't in a human language. Still, we caught the name."

"And trust me when I say that if you ask everyone who was present at that moment, they will all mention the same name, even though he had said dozens of others. It became quite amusing at one point." She laughed wholeheartedly. Ishan looked at her in stunned silence. "So, my question is, do you know who this person is?"

"I... what? You didn't tell me the name," Ishan responded, confused. "Oh. Right, I didn't." She paused, staring at him, clearly expecting an answer. "You want me to tell you the name?" she said, sounding a bit let down as if it should have been obvious.

"Yeah," Ishan replied, feeling paranoid about what she would say. "It was 'Ishan.' Do you know someone by that name?" Her smile was no longer sarcastic; it felt sweet, mixed with something Ishan couldn't quite identify. Before he could process her words, she quietly left the room.
